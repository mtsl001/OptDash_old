Option Buying
Dashboard v2.0

A real-time analytics platform for systematic NSE index option buying. It pulls live market data from BigQuery every five minutes, processes it through a quantitative analytics layer, and surfaces actionable signals across eight dashboard panels — from gamma exposure to slippage-adjusted strike selection.

📊 NIFTY · BANKNIFTY · FINNIFTY · MIDCPNIFTY · NIFTYNXT50 ⚡ 5-min live refresh 🏦 BigQuery → DuckDB → React

Terminal 1 — Pipeline

Pulls raw data from BigQuery every 5 minutes. Writes it as Parquet files to disk. The only process that touches BigQuery.

⚡
Terminal 2 — API

FastAPI server reads the Parquet files via DuckDB. Computes GEX, CoC, PCR, S_score and serves results as JSON at :8000.

🖥
Terminal 3 — Dashboard

Vite + React frontend. Polls the API every 5 seconds. Renders all eight panels. Open at localhost:5173.

Data Flow in Detail

Every five minutes during NSE market hours (09:16:30 → 15:30 IST), the pipeline runs this cycle:

  1. Load watermark
    Read data/watermark.json — the timestamp of the last successfully pulled record. On the very first run after backfill this will be 2026-02-20 15:30:XX.
  2. Pull incremental rows from BigQuery
    Query: SELECT * FROM upxtx WHERE record_time > {watermark}. Typically returns ~1,600 rows per 5-min snapshot covering all index option contracts.
  3. Validate
    Check schema, coalesce nulls in ltp using close_price then close. Correct any negative IV values. Log row counts by instrument type.
  4. Load ATM window
    Read today's ATM windows from data/processed/atm_windows/YYYY-MM-DD.json. If it's the first pull of the day, compute them from the 09:15 snapshot's opening spot price.
  5. Compute derived columns
    Add: gex_k, coc, moneyness_pct, oi_delta, expiry_tier, in_atm_window, obi_raw, and more.
  6. Write Parquet
    Save as data/raw/options/trade_date=YYYY-MM-DD/UNDERLYING_HHMM.parquet. Row group size = 100,000 for maximum DuckDB parallelism.
  7. Refresh DuckDB views + update watermark
    DuckDB glob views auto-discover the new file. Watermark advances to the latest record_time in the just-written batch. Atomically saved to data/watermark.json.

Panel D: Strike Screener

The Strike Screener ranks all option contracts within the ATM window (±8 strikes) by a slippage-adjusted efficiency score called S_score. Higher S_score = better bang for your premium. The screener runs at the 09:20 snapshot by default — the first stable reading of the day.

Each row shows: expiry, option type (CE/PE), strike, LTP, IV, delta, theta, slippage penalty (ρ), and the final S_score. Stars (★★★★) give a quick visual rank.

💡 How to use it: Sort by S_score. Look for strikes with ρ < 0.10 (low slippage), IV between 10–25%, and delta between 0.25–0.45. These are your candidate trades. Cross-reference with the Environment Gate before entering.

Panel E: PCR Divergence

Retail Trap (Bear)

Concept Library

Every metric on the dashboard is grounded in options market microstructure theory. This section explains each concept from first principles — what it measures, how it is calculated, and what the signal means for a buyer of options.

Γ

Gamma Exposure (GEX)

MARKET STRUCTURE · DEALER HEDGING

Every time you buy an option from a market maker, they are now short that option. To remain delta-neutral (not taking a directional view), they must hedge by buying or selling the underlying. Gamma tells you how much their hedge changes as the spot price moves. GEX aggregates this across all open contracts to give you a market-wide picture of how much buying or selling dealers will do when price moves.

Think of positive GEX as a gravitational force that pulls price back toward the strike with the highest concentration of open interest. When GEX is large and positive, every price move away from equilibrium triggers dealers to trade against it — creating a "pinning" effect. When GEX is negative or collapses, that gravity disappears and directional moves become self-sustaining.

Formula — per contract contribution
GEX_k = Gamma × OI × Spot² × 0.01 × d_dir

d_dir = +1 for Call options (dealers are short calls, long gamma)
d_dir = −1 for Put options (dealers are short puts, short gamma)

The dashboard then sums all GEX_k values across every strike and expiry in Tier 1 (trading expiries) to get Net GEX in ₹ Billions for that snapshot.

Positive GEX
Dealers long gamma. They sell rallies and buy dips. Market chops. Avoid momentum strategies.
Negative GEX
Dealers short gamma. They must buy strength and sell weakness. Market self-reinforces moves. Ideal for option buyers.
GEX Collapse (<70%)
Even while positive, a sharp decline removes pinning support. Trending moves start here. Environment Gate scores 2 points.
💡 Practical rule: The best option buying setups occur when GEX was large in the morning and then drops sharply (≥30% from peak) by mid-morning. The "pin" is lifting. Combined with V_CoC < −10, this is a very high-probability setup.
₹

Cost of Carry (CoC) & Velocity

FUTURES MARKET · INSTITUTIONAL FOOTPRINT

In a fair market, futures trade at a premium above spot: Futures = Spot + (Spot × risk-free rate × DTE/365). This premium is called the Cost of Carry. For NIFTY near-month futures, the "fair" CoC is typically +30 to +80 points.

When large institutional players (FIIs, prop desks) aggressively sell or buy futures, they push the price away from this fair value. CoC moving sharply negative (futures falling into discount) means heavy institutional selling — a signal retail traders almost never see in time.

Cost of Carry
CoC = Futures LTP − Underlying Spot

15-minute Velocity
V_CoC = CoC(t) − CoC(t−3)    # 3 × 5-min bars = 15 minutes
V_CoC < −10
Institutional short campaign. FIIs selling ≥₹1,000 Cr equivalent in futures within 15 minutes. High confidence PE signal.
CoC in Discount
Futures below spot. Sellers dominating. Markets with sustained discount trend lower.
V_CoC > +10
Institutional long campaign. FIIs buying futures aggressively. CE signal.

Why it leads price by 60–90 minutes: Institutions build futures positions first (lower impact cost), then options flow follows as their delta hedges are placed. By the time price moves, you already know from V_CoC.

σ

Implied Volatility (IV)

OPTIONS PRICING · FEAR GAUGE

An option's price depends on how much the market expects the underlying to move before expiry. Implied Volatility is the market's consensus forecast of annualised standard deviation, derived by reverse-engineering the Black-Scholes model from the observed option price.

If NIFTY is at 25,000 and ATM IV is 14%, the market expects NIFTY to move approximately ±14% × √(DTE/365) by expiry. For a 7-day option: 25,000 × 14% × √(7/365) ≈ ±488 points.

Daily expected move from ATM IV
Daily Move = Spot × IV% / √252

Example: 25,000 × 0.14 / √252 ≈ ±220 points per day

As an option buyer, IV is your enemy when high and your friend when low. When you buy high-IV options, you need the underlying to move more than the market expects to profit. When IV is low, even moderate moves yield profit.

IV Low (IVP <30%)
Options are cheap relative to history. Buying is favourable — you pay less for the same probability of profit.
IV High (IVP >75%)
Options are expensive. You need an outsized move to profit. Consider debit spreads instead of naked longs.

IV Skew

In index options, put IVs are almost always higher than equivalent call IVs (the "volatility skew"). This reflects institutional demand for downside protection. The IV Surface panel shows this visually — PE strikes will be brighter (higher IV) than equidistant CE strikes.

%

IV Rank (IVR) & IV Percentile (IVP)

VOLATILITY CONTEXT · BUYING TIMING

Knowing that IV is at 14% tells you nothing without context. Is 14% cheap or expensive for NIFTY? IVR and IVP answer that by placing today's IV within its historical range.

IV Rank (IVR) — position within range
IVR = (IV_today − IV_52wk_low) / (IV_52wk_high − IV_52wk_low) × 100

IV Percentile (IVP) — percent of days below today
IVP = % of past 90 days where closing IV was lower than today's IV
IVR: position in range

IVR = 0% means IV is at its 90-day low. IVR = 100% means IV is at its 90-day high. IVR = 50% means IV is exactly halfway between its recent extremes.

Weakness: One or two extreme days distort the entire range. If IV spiked to 35% during a crash and is now at 15%, IVR might read 10% even though 15% is still elevated for normal conditions.

IVP: percentile

IVP = 25% means IV was lower than today on 75% of the past 90 days — today is relatively cheap. IVP = 80% means today is more expensive than 80% of recent days.

Advantage: Not distorted by outliers. More robust measure. Dashboard uses IVP for Environment Gate condition 5.

IVPSignalAction for option buyers
<20%CHEAPStrong buy signal on volatility. Very favourable entry for option buyers.
20–50%FAIRReasonable entry. Score 2 points in Environment Gate.
50–75%EXPENSIVECaution. Use spreads or wait for IV to compress.
>75%VERY EXPENSIVEAvoid naked long options. IV likely to revert lower.
P/C

Put-Call Ratio (PCR) & Divergence

SENTIMENT ANALYSIS · RETAIL vs INSTITUTIONAL

The Put-Call Ratio compares the activity in put options versus call options. A ratio above 1 means more put activity than call activity — traditionally interpreted as bearish sentiment. But the dashboard goes deeper by tracking two separate PCRs and comparing them.

PCR Volume — fast, retail sentiment
PCR_Vol = Σ PE Volume / Σ CE Volume   (current 5-min snapshot)

PCR OI — slow, institutional positioning
PCR_OI = Σ PE Open Interest / Σ CE Open Interest   (accumulated)

PCR Divergence — the signal
Divergence = PCR_Vol − PCR_OI

Why they diverge: Open Interest accumulates over days as institutions build positions patiently. Volume spikes intraday as retail traders react to news. A sudden retail rush to buy puts (PCR_Vol spikes) while institutions remain relatively neutral in OI (PCR_OI steady) creates a divergence — and historically, this retail panic marks a local low, not a continuing sell-off.

Divergence > +0.30
Retail panic-buying puts. Institutions unmoved. Classic retail trap. Spot tends to bounce within 1–3 candles. Environment Gate scores 1 point.
Divergence < −0.20
Retail panic-buying calls while institutions hold puts. Spot tends to fall. Favour PE buying.
Divergence −0.20 to +0.25
Retail and institutional flows aligned. No strong trap signal. Use other indicators.
Δ

Option Greeks

OPTIONS PRICING SENSITIVITY

The Greeks measure how sensitive an option's price is to different factors. All are pre-computed by the BigQuery data source and used throughout the dashboard.

GreekMeasuresWhat it means for an option buyerTypical range
Delta (Δ) Price change per 1-point spot move An ATM option has Δ ≈ 0.50. If NIFTY moves +100 pts, the option gains ≈₹50. Delta rises toward 1.0 as option goes deep ITM. 0 to 1 (CE), 0 to −1 (PE)
Gamma (Γ) Rate of change of Delta per 1-point spot move High gamma = your delta accelerates on favourable moves. ATM options have the highest gamma. Collapses rapidly as DTE approaches zero. 0 to 0.005 typically
Theta (Θ) Daily value decay The cost of holding the option overnight. If Theta = −6.63, your option loses ₹6.63 per day purely from time passing, all else equal. Accelerates in the last 7 days before expiry. −0.5 to −30 for near-expiry ATM
Vega (ν) Price change per 1% change in IV If IV rises from 14% to 15%, a Vega of 12 means the option gains ₹12. Option buyers benefit from IV expansion, hurt by IV contraction. 5 to 50 for index ATM options
Rho (ρ) Sensitivity to interest rates Minor for short-dated options. Not prominently displayed. Note: "ρ" in the Strike Screener refers to the slippage penalty, not this Rho. Very small — typically ignored

Taylor Series P&L Decomposition

The Position Monitor uses these Greeks to break down why your P&L changed. For any time interval:

ΔV ≈ Δ·ΔS + ½Γ·(ΔS)² + ν·Δσ + Θ·Δt

where:
ΔS = change in spot price
Δσ = change in implied volatility
Δt = time elapsed (fraction of trading day)
S★

Slippage-Adjusted S_score

STRIKE SELECTION · EFFICIENCY RANKING

The S_score answers the question: "Of all available strikes, which gives me the best combination of directional exposure, time decay resistance, and liquidity?" It is the primary ranking criterion in the Strike Screener.

Efficiency Ratio
EffRatio = |Delta| / |Theta|    # directional gain per unit of daily decay cost

Slippage Penalty (ρ)
ρ = exp(−2 × TBQ × TSQ / ((TBQ + TSQ) × V_bench))

# ρ → 0 when order book is balanced and deep (low slippage)
# ρ → 1 when one side dominates or book is thin (high slippage)

Final S_score
S_score = EffRatio × (1 − ρ)

The slippage penalty ρ uses total_buy_qty and total_sell_qty from the order book. When both sides are deep and balanced, ρ approaches zero and S_score equals the raw efficiency ratio. When the book is thin or one-sided, ρ approaches 1 and S_score collapses to near zero — warning you away from illiquid strikes that look attractive on paper but will cost you dearly at execution.

⚠️ V_bench = 2,000,000: This is the benchmark order book size for index options. Strikes where TBQ or TSQ are far below this will be penalised. For stock futures (which have thinner books), this parameter is adjusted separately.
⚖

Order Book Imbalance (OBI)

MICROSTRUCTURE · IMMEDIATE PRESSURE

The Order Book Imbalance measures the real-time balance of buying versus selling pressure in the order book. Unlike volume (which is historical), OBI is a leading indicator — it shows what is queued to happen, not what already happened.

OBI = (Total Buy Quantity − Total Sell Quantity) / (Total Buy Quantity + Total Sell Quantity)

Range: −1 to +1
+1 = all bids, no asks (extreme buy pressure)
−1 = all asks, no bids (extreme sell pressure)
0 = perfectly balanced

The dashboard applies a 5-snapshot (25-minute) rolling average to remove noise. This Smoothed OBI is what feeds the Environment Gate condition 6.

Smoothed OBI < −0.15
Sustained sell-side pressure in the order book. Sellers are more aggressive. Consistent with a downward trend.
Smoothed OBI > +0.15
Buy-side pressure dominant. Buyers paying up. Consistent with upward momentum.

OBI is also computed for futures separately. The "Futures BS Ratio" in the Environment Gate is derived from futures OBI — when futures OBI is negative, it means institutions are placing sell orders more aggressively than buy orders, a bearish signal.

SL

Theta-Adjusted Trailing Stop Loss

RISK MANAGEMENT · POSITION SIZING

A conventional stop loss (e.g., "exit if premium falls 30% from entry") ignores the fact that option premiums naturally decay through time, even if the spot price doesn't move. This creates a dilemma: a tight SL will frequently stop you out of perfectly valid positions due to theta decay alone.

The Theta-Adjusted SL solves this by lowering the stop loss floor in proportion to time elapsed. As the day progresses and theta eats into your premium, your stop loss adjusts downward to account for that expected decay — so you are only stopped out by an adverse spot move, not by the passage of time.

Base Stop Loss (fixed at entry)
SL_base = Entry Premium × (1 − Max Loss Fraction)
Example: ₹19.95 × (1 − 0.30) = ₹13.97

Theta-Adjusted SL at time t
SL_adj(t) = SL_base + (Θ_daily × t_elapsed_minutes / 375)

# 375 = total NSE trading minutes (09:15 to 15:30)
# Θ_daily is negative, so SL_adj moves lower over time

Example: You enter a PE at ₹19.95 with Θ = −6.63/day. By noon (180 minutes elapsed out of 375), the expected decay is: −6.63 × 180/375 = −3.18 points. So your SL moves from ₹13.97 down to ₹13.97 + (−3.18) = ₹10.79. If the PE is at ₹22 at noon, you're well above the SL — the trade is working.

SL above Entry Price
"Guaranteed Profit Zone" — as the position appreciates, trailing SL eventually locks in a profit no matter what happens next.
STOP_HIT
Current LTP dropped below SL_adj. The adverse spot move exceeded what theta decay alone would cause. Exit immediately.

Expiry Tiers

EXPIRY CLASSIFICATION · LIQUIDITY FRAMEWORK

Not all expiries are equal. The dashboard classifies every option contract into one of three tiers based on days-to-expiry (DTE). Only Tier 1 contracts are used for GEX computation and strike selection.

TierDTE RangePurposeExamples (from NIFTY weekly chain)
TIER1_NEAR DTE ≤ 12 Primary trading expiry. Highest liquidity, tightest spreads, sharpest gamma response to spot moves. Used for GEX, CoC, PCR, and strike selection. Current week expiry, next week expiry
TIER1_FAR DTE ≥ 35 Secondary trading expiry. Used for GEX and for term structure slope computation. Lower gamma but more stable theta profile. Monthly expiry, next monthly
TIER2_IV_ONLY 13 ≤ DTE < 35 Liquidity gap. Not suitable for trading (wide spreads, low OI). Used only for IV term structure charting to fill in the forward curve. Biweekly or mid-month expiries
ATM

ATM Window

STRIKE UNIVERSE · DAILY ANCHOR

The ATM (At-The-Money) Window defines which strikes the dashboard shows for each underlying. It is anchored to the day-open spot price, not the current price — this ensures the strike universe remains consistent throughout the trading day regardless of where the spot has moved.

ATM Strike computation (from 09:15 snapshot)
ATM = ROUND(Day_Open_Spot / Strike_Interval) × Strike_Interval

Window = [ATM − 8 × Interval, ATM + 8 × Interval]

NIFTY (Interval=50):   17 strikes over 800-point range
BANKNIFTY (Interval=100): 17 strikes over 1,600-point range
MIDCPNIFTY (Interval=25): 17 strikes over 400-point range

This ±8 strike window contains the vast majority of liquid options (usually 95%+ of volume). Strikes outside this window are pulled from BigQuery but excluded from dashboard displays and analytics.

ℹ️ If NIFTY gaps up 300 points at open, the ATM window shifts accordingly — anchoring to the actual opening price, not yesterday's close. The window file for the day is saved at data/processed/atm_windows/YYYY-MM-DD.json.

Trading Workflow

The dashboard is designed around a structured trading day. Here is the recommended workflow from pre-market to close.

Phase 1: Pre-Market (09:00–09:15)

  1. Open all three terminal tabs and start the stack (pipeline → API → dashboard)
  2. Verify the dashboard loads at localhost:5173 with no error panels
  3. Check yesterday's data is visible by selecting the previous date in the date picker
  4. Note yesterday's closing GEX — if it closed near a key level, today may see continuation or reversal
  5. Check IVP from the screener — if <30%, you enter today with a structural edge

Phase 2: Market Open & First Signals (09:15–09:45)

  1. 09:15: Market opens. First snapshot hits BigQuery within 60 seconds. Pipeline pulls it at 09:16:30.
  2. 09:16:30: Dashboard updates. ATM window is now computed from the opening spot price. Environment Gate shows first score — typically low (only 1–2 conditions met this early).
  3. 09:20: Watch CoC and V_CoC. If V_CoC is already below −10 on the first reading, a significant bearish institutional move was placed at or before open — high alert.
  4. 09:20–09:35: Strike Screener runs on the 09:20 snapshot. This is your primary strike selection window. Note the top 5 strikes by S_score for CE and PE separately.
  5. 09:35–09:45: Wait for Environment Gate to reach 4+ points before entering. If it has not reached 4 by 09:45, extend the wait until 10:00–10:15.
⚠️ First 15-minute rule: Never enter a trade in the first 15 minutes of the session (09:15–09:30). The opening auction creates artificial liquidity. Wait for at least 2–3 five-minute candles to form before reading market structure.

Phase 3: In the Trade (09:45–14:30)

  1. Enter the bought option into the Position Monitor panel (expiry, strike, type, entry LTP, theta)
  2. The theta-adjusted SL is now computed and plotted automatically every 5 minutes
  3. Check the Alert Feed after every 5-min refresh — any new COC_VELOCITY or GEX_DECLINE alerts?
  4. Monitor the PCR Divergence panel — if divergence reverses strongly against your position, consider partial exit
  5. At 100% profit (LTP = 2× entry): the Position Monitor shows PROFIT_100PCT_PARTIAL_EXIT. Consider booking 50% and letting the rest run with the theta SL protecting profits.

Phase 4: Exit Rules

EXIT 1
Stop Loss Hit: Position Monitor shows STOP_HIT in red. LTP has dropped below the theta-adjusted SL. Exit immediately at market. Do not wait for a bounce.
EXIT 2
Signal Reversal: Environment Gate drops from GO to WAIT or NO-GO. The setup that triggered your entry is no longer valid. Consider exiting 50–75% of position.
EXIT 3
Profit Target: LTP reaches 2× entry (100% gain). Book 50% profit. Move remaining position to trailing mode with SL raised to entry price.
EXIT 4
Time Exit: Always exit by 14:30 IST regardless of P&L. Theta decay accelerates sharply in the final hour. Risk-reward deteriorates significantly after 14:30 for near-expiry options.

Troubleshooting

🔴

Dashboard shows no data / empty panels

MOST COMMON ISSUE

Cause 1: The API server is not running.
Fix: Open Tab 2 and run python run_api.py. Verify at http://127.0.0.1:8000/docs.

Cause 2: No Parquet data on disk yet (backfill hasn't run).
Fix: Run python run_pipeline.py in Tab 1. Wait for the backfill to complete (2–5 min). Then refresh the dashboard.

Cause 3: Wrong date selected in the dashboard date picker.
Fix: Change the date to a day in the backfilled range (2026-02-17 to 2026-02-20).

Times appear wrong — shifted by 5 hours 30 minutes
