Expiry Tiers

LIQUIDITY CLASSIFICATION · ANALYTICS SCOPE · TRADING UNIVERSE

NSE simultaneously lists option contracts for multiple expiry dates — weekly, biweekly, monthly, and quarterly. Not all expiries have the same liquidity, analytical relevance, or trading suitability. The dashboard classifies every contract into one of three tiers based on Days-to-Expiry (DTE), and applies tier-specific inclusion rules across all panels.

TierDTEDashboard UseWhy This RangeTypical Contracts
TIER1_NEAR ≤ 12 days Everything: GEX, CoC, PCR, Strike Screener, S_score, Position Monitor, IV Surface. Primary trading universe. Highest liquidity. Tightest bid-ask spreads. Sharpest gamma response to spot moves. Theta decay rapid enough to matter for intraday SL calculations. Current week NIFTY weekly expiry, next week expiry.
TIER1_FAR ≥ 35 days GEX (included in total Net GEX), IV term structure charting, CoC term structure comparison. Not used for S_score or Strike Screener. Enough liquidity for GEX computation to be meaningful. More stable theta profile. Spreads acceptable for monthly options. Provides "anchor" GEX contrast with near-expiry. Near-month expiry (last Thursday of month), next monthly. Index monthly series.
TIER2_IV_ONLY 13–34 days IV term structure and IV surface charts only. Excluded from all trading analytics. Not in GEX, screener, or PCR. Liquidity gap zone. Wide bid-ask spreads, low OI, unreliable GEX contribution. Including in screener would surface illiquid strikes that look attractive on paper but are poor to trade. Mid-month or biweekly expiries falling in this DTE window. Chart-only.

Tier Assignment Is Dynamic

DTE = (expiry_date − trade_date).days

Tier re-evaluated daily as DTE counts down.
Example: A monthly expiry 40 days out = TIER1_FAR
Same expiry 20 days out = TIER2_IV_ONLY
Same expiry 8 days out = TIER1_NEAR (enters active trading universe)
Trade in TIER1_NEAR
Maximum liquidity. Sub-₹1 spreads on ATM NIFTY options typical. S_score slippage penalty ρ will be very low. All entry signals use this tier.
Avoid TIER2 for Trading
Even if S_score looks attractive, TIER2 strikes have 5–15 point spreads. The slippage penalty ρ will be high, correctly suppressing the score and warning you away.
Expiry Day (DTE = 0)
Do not buy options of that expiry. Gamma is extreme, theta collapse accelerates after noon, spreads widen sharply. S_score collapses automatically on DTE=0 due to extreme theta denominator. Treat as exit-only.
ATM

ATM Window

STRIKE UNIVERSE · DAY-OPEN ANCHOR · DAILY CONSISTENCY

The ATM Window defines the exact set of strikes included in all dashboard analytics for each underlying every day. It is anchored to the day-opening spot price from the 09:15 snapshot — not the current live price. This ensures the strike universe is completely consistent from 09:15 to 15:30, making cross-snapshot comparisons meaningful.

Step 1 — Day-open spot from first snapshot
Day_Open_Spot = underlying_spot from earliest record_time on trade_date

Step 2 — Round to nearest strike interval
ATM_Strike = ROUND(Day_Open_Spot / Strike_Interval) × Strike_Interval

Step 3 — Window bounds (±8 strikes)
Lower = ATM_Strike − (8 × Strike_Interval)
Upper = ATM_Strike + (8 × Strike_Interval)

NIFTY (interval=50): 17 strikes, 800-point range
BANKNIFTY (interval=100): 17 strikes, 1,600-point range
FINNIFTY (interval=50): 17 strikes, 800-point range
MIDCPNIFTY (interval=25): 17 strikes, 400-point range
NIFTYNXT50 (interval=50): 17 strikes, 800-point range

The window file is computed from the first pull of each day and saved immediately to data/processed/atm_windows/YYYY-MM-DD.json. It is never updated mid-day — the same fixed window applies from 09:15:00 to 15:30:00.

Example: data/processed/atm_windows/2026-02-23.json
{"NIFTY": {"atm": 25000, "lower": 24600, "upper": 25400, "interval": 50},
 "BANKNIFTY": {"atm": 52000, "lower": 51200, "upper": 52800, "interval": 100},
 "FINNIFTY": {"atm": 23500, "lower": 23100, "upper": 23900, "interval": 50}}
💡Gap-up example: NIFTY closed Friday at 25,000 but opens Monday at 25,600 (+600 gap). The Monday ATM window anchors at 25,600, spanning 25,200 to 26,000. The entire strike set shifts up 600 points. This is correct — the relevant strikes for Monday are centred on Monday's actual open, not Friday's close.
M%

Moneyness

STRIKE POSITION RELATIVE TO SPOT · CROSS-UNDERLYING COMPARABILITY

Moneyness expresses where a strike sits relative to the current spot price as a percentage. It allows comparison of option positions across different underlyings on a common scale — a 2% OTM NIFTY option is comparable to a 2% OTM BANKNIFTY option even though the raw strikes differ by tens of thousands of points.

Moneyness% = (Strike_Price − Underlying_Spot) / Underlying_Spot × 100

For Calls (CE): Positive moneyness = OTM, Negative = ITM
For Puts (PE): Negative moneyness = OTM, Positive = ITM

ATM: Moneyness% ≈ 0%, Delta CE ≈ +0.50, Delta PE ≈ −0.50
1% OTM CE: Moneyness = +1%, Delta CE ≈ 0.35
1% OTM PE: Moneyness = −1%, Delta PE ≈ −0.35
|Moneyness|ZoneCE/PE DeltaBest Use
<0.5%ATM0.45–0.55Max liquidity, max gamma. Best for short-duration intraday plays.
0.5%–2%Near OTM0.25–0.45Best S_score zone. Higher EffRatio. Lower premium than ATM. Recommended for most screener selections.
2%–4%OTM0.10–0.25Cheaper premium, needs larger move. Higher slippage penalty typical.
>4%Far OTM<0.10Very low probability. Usually filtered out by ATM window (±8 strikes ≈ ±2–4%).

Watermark — Incremental Pull State

STATE MANAGEMENT · IDEMPOTENCY · CRASH RESILIENCE

The watermark is a single timestamp in data/watermark.json recording the last moment successfully pulled from BigQuery. Every incremental pull queries for records newer than this timestamp, then advances it to the latest record time in the new batch.

This provides three critical properties: (1) No duplicates — the same records are never pulled twice. (2) No gaps — every record between old and new watermark is always captured. (3) Crash resilience — if the pipeline crashes mid-pull before writing Parquet, the watermark has not advanced yet, so the next run re-pulls that batch safely.

Watermark file format
{"watermark": "2026-02-23 09:21:00"}   # IST, no timezone suffix, no Z, no +05:30

BigQuery pull query using watermark
SELECT * FROM `universal-ion-437606-b7.bgquery.upxtx`
WHERE record_time > TIMESTAMP("2026-02-23 09:21:00")
ORDER BY record_time ASC

Watermark saved atomically (macOS APFS)
Write to data/watermark.json.tmp → Path.replace() → data/watermark.json
⚠️Never manually edit watermark.json unless you know exactly what you are doing. Setting it backward causes duplicate data; setting it forward creates a gap in historical data. To diagnose a suspected gap, compare Parquet filenames (HHMM) against the watermark timestamp.

GEX Regime States

FOUR MARKET STRUCTURE REGIMES · DEALER BEHAVIOUR CLASSIFICATION

The dashboard classifies market structure into four distinct GEX regimes. The regime is determined by the sign of Net GEX and its trajectory relative to the day's peak.

RegimeConditionDealer BehaviourMarket EffectOption Buyer Strategy
POSITIVE_CHOP GEX >0, >80% of peak Long gamma. Sell rallies, buy dips actively. Mean-reverting. IV compressed. Range-bound. Options bleed premium. Avoid momentum plays. Wait for regime to change. Use the time to study signals.
POSITIVE_WEAKENING GEX >0, 50–80% of peak Long gamma but hedging force decaying. Reducing positions. Range beginning to crack. Direction uncertain. Volatility picking up. Gate starts scoring (Condition 1 hits). Watch for confirmation signals. Prepare entry candidates from screener.
POSITIVE_COLLAPSING GEX >0, <50% of peak Rapidly unwinding hedges. Pinning support almost gone. Breakout in progress or imminent. Volume surge typical. Large candles forming. Best buying conditions. Both GEX Gate points (2 pts) fully scored. High-conviction entry.
NEGATIVE_TREND GEX <0 Short gamma. Must buy strength and sell weakness — amplifies every move. Strong trending. Self-reinforcing momentum. Moves exceed normal expectations. High volatility. Most aggressive buying opportunity. Trend-following with wide initial SL. Be alert for sharp reversals when GEX flips back positive.
💡Most important transition: POSITIVE_WEAKENING → POSITIVE_COLLAPSING (GEX crosses below 70% of peak). This transition — visible on the GEX % gauge in real-time — frequently precedes the day's main directional move by 1–3 five-minute bars. Combined with V_CoC <−10, this is the highest-conviction setup the dashboard produces.

Volume Velocity

UNUSUAL ACTIVITY DETECTION · INFORMED FLOW IDENTIFICATION

Volume Velocity measures how current options volume compares to the session baseline established in the first 60 minutes of trading. Sudden volume acceleration — especially in specific strikes — often precedes large price moves and may indicate informed institutional flow.

Baseline (average of 09:20–10:15 snapshots)
V_baseline = Mean(volume_09:20, volume_09:25, ..., volume_10:15)

Volume Velocity ratio at time t
Vol_Velocity(t) = snapshot_volume(t) / V_baseline

Ratio 1.0 = exactly at baseline (normal activity)
Ratio 2.0 = 2× baseline → VOLUME_SPIKE alert triggered in Alert Feed
Ratio 3.0+ = extreme activity, likely institutional block trade or news catalyst
VOLUME_SPIKE Alert (Ratio >2)
Check: is the spike in CE or PE strikes? Is it concentrated in one strike (informed) or spread across many (retail panic)?
PE Spike + V_CoC <−10
Dual confirmation of institutional bearish flow. Highest conviction PE setup. Rare but extremely reliable when it occurs.
CE Spike + V_CoC >+10
Dual confirmation of institutional bullish flow. Highest conviction CE setup.
Volume Spike Alone
Without confirming GEX or CoC signal, a volume spike may be retail noise. Do not act on volume alone.

Trading Workflow — Six Phases

The dashboard is built around a structured daily routine. Consistency in following this sequence matters more than any individual signal. The system's edge comes from discipline applied over many trades, not from any single perfect call.

ℹ️Verify your Mac clock shows IST before each trading day: run date in Terminal. The pipeline's market-hours detection uses local system time — if it is off by even one minute, the first pull may be missed.
PHASE 1

Pre-Market Setup (09:00–09:15 IST)

The 15 minutes before market open establish your context for the day. Do not enter the session without reviewing yesterday's conditions and setting your bias.

  • Open Terminal tabs 1, 2, 3. Start pipeline → API → dashboard in that order. Wait 60 seconds between each for initialisation.
  • Verify at :8000/docs (15 endpoints visible) and :5173 (panels load with no error banners).
  • Select yesterday's date. Review closing GEX trend: rising into close (pinned = chop expected tomorrow morning) or collapsing (directional continuation possible)?
  • Note yesterday's closing CoC: in premium (neutral) or in discount (sellers dominated into close)? Discount CoC into yesterday's close = bearish carry-over today.
  • Check IVP level: <30% → structural buying advantage today. >70% → plan debit spreads, not naked longs.
  • Check pre-market macro: SGX NIFTY direction, US overnight close, RBI or major news. Large gap expectations shift ATM window significantly at open.
  • Set a directional prior: CE-biased, PE-biased, or neutral. Write it down. The dashboard will confirm or contradict it — both are useful information.
PHASE 2

Market Open — Information Gathering (09:15–09:45 IST)

This phase is observation-only. Do not take positions. The opening 30 minutes resolve the overnight gap, establish the day's GEX baseline, and reveal whether institutional flow is directional or balanced.

09:15:00
Market Opens
NSE auction clears. First prices available. Options spreads widest right now — avoid activity. Watch the gap direction and opening volume.
09:16:30 → 09:16:40
First Pipeline Pull → Dashboard Updates
Opening snapshot captured and processed. ATM window computed and saved. All panels show live data for the first time today. Check: Is CoC already in discount? What is V_CoC on first reading? What is the opening GEX level?
09:21:30
Second Pull — Gate First Meaningful Reading
Environment Gate now has two snapshots of data. First meaningful score appears. Smoothed OBI begins accumulating. Check the Gate score: if it is already 5+ at this point with V_CoC <−10, an institutional campaign started at open — high-alert session.
09:26:30
Third Pull — Screener Stabilises
S_scores stabilise with three data points. The Strike Screener now shows reliable rankings. Note your top 3–5 candidates for CE and PE separately. You won't have time to research them when the Gate flips GO.
09:30–09:45
Watch GEX % of Peak
This is the metric to track during Phase 2. Is it declining from the opening reading? Has V_CoC crossed −10? Is PCR divergence building? The Gate will turn GO only when multiple conditions align.
⚠️First-15-minute rule: Never execute a trade between 09:15 and 09:30. ATM NIFTY options spreads can be ₹3–8 wide at open versus ₹0.25–0.50 by 09:30. That spread alone is a 15–40% penalty on a ₹20 option. Wait.
PHASE 3

Trade Entry (09:30–10:30 IST)

Once the Gate turns GO (≥4 pts), entry decisions should take under two minutes. The analysis is done — Phase 2 prepared your candidate strikes. The only decision now is which strike from your pre-screened list to buy, and how many lots.

Entry Decision: Gate Score → Lot Size

Gate ScoreV_CoCActionSize
7–9<−10 or >+10Maximum confidence directional entry100% of planned lots
5–6ConfirmingStrong confidence entry75% of planned lots
4NeutralBorderline GO — minimal entry50% of planned lots
2–3AnyWAIT — no entry0%
0–1AnyNO-GO — absolutely no entry0%

Strike Selection Sequence

  1. Filter Strike Screener by direction determined by Gate: V_CoC <0 → PE, V_CoC >0 → CE, PCR signal → confirms
  2. Sort by S_score descending. Review top 5 candidates.
  3. Eliminate any with slippage penalty ρ >0.15 (thin order book — execution risk)
  4. Eliminate any with IV >25% (expensive volatility — unfavourable entry)
  5. Eliminate any with delta <0.20 (too far OTM — low probability of profit)
  6. Buy the strike with the highest remaining S_score
  7. Immediately enter the position into the Position Monitor panel before the next 5-minute refresh
💡The screener LTP may be 60–90 seconds old. When you execute, check the current market price. If the premium has already moved significantly from the screener value (e.g., more than 10%), the S_score has degraded — either accept the new price or skip to the next candidate.
PHASE 4

In the Trade (Entry → 14:30 IST)

Once in a position, the goal is disciplined monitoring with minimal interference. The theta-adjusted SL is doing its job. Your role is to watch for system-level signals that require action, not to micromanage the P&L with every price tick.

Every 5-Minute Refresh — Monitoring Checklist

Profit Booking Protocol

+50% Gain
LTP = 1.5× entry. Optional partial booking (25% of lots) if signals are weakening. Not required if Gate score is still high and directional signals confirm.
+100% Gain
Book 50% of remaining lots at market. Raise SL on remaining lots to your original entry LTP. Position Monitor shows PROFIT_100PCT_PARTIAL_EXIT. You are now in a free trade — maximum loss from this point is ₹0.
+150%–200%
Continue trailing with theta-adjusted SL. Consider booking another 25% of the original position at each 50% increment. Keep the last tranche running until time exit at 14:30 or STOP_HIT. Extraordinary gains (3–5×) are possible when GEX regime stays NEGATIVE_TREND throughout the session.

Handling a Contra-Signal While In the Trade

Example: You are long PE (bearish) and PCR Divergence suddenly spikes to +0.35 (retail panic-buying puts = contrarian bounce signal). This contradicts your position. How to handle:

  • If PE has not yet hit its SL and is in profit: hold. A contra-signal is not a stop loss hit. The theta SL is protecting you.
  • If PE is profitable (+30%+) and a strong contra-signal appears: consider booking 50% to lock gains before a potential bounce against you.
  • If Gate drops to 0–1 and a strong contra-signal appears simultaneously: exit the full position. Both structural edge and sentiment have reversed.
PHASE 5

Exit Rules — Strict Priority Order

Exit rules are enforced in the order listed. Rule 1 always overrides Rules 2–5. Never override Rule 1 for any reason — it is a hard system rule, not a discretionary guideline.

RULE 1
Stop Loss Hit (absolute, non-negotiable): Position Monitor shows STOP_HIT in red. LTP dropped below SL_adj. Exit the entire remaining position at market immediately. No waiting for a bounce. No averaging down. No exceptions. The system has confirmed the directional thesis is wrong. This is the most important discipline in the entire workflow.
RULE 2
Environment Gate Collapse: Gate drops from GO to NO-GO (0–1 pts) while in a position. All structural conditions that justified your entry have reversed. Exit 75–100% of position immediately. The edge is gone entirely.
RULE 3
Gate Weakening: Gate drops from GO to WAIT (2–3 pts). Exit 50% of position. Raise SL on remaining 50% to entry LTP (free trade). The edge has diminished but has not reversed completely.
RULE 4
100% Profit Target: LTP reaches 2× entry. Book 50% of lots at market. Raise SL on remaining lots to entry LTP. This locks in your profit and creates a risk-free remainder.
RULE 5
Time Exit at 14:30 IST (hard deadline): Close all remaining positions by 14:30 regardless of P&L. Theta decay accelerates sharply in the final 60 minutes for near-expiry options. The risk-reward of holding deteriorates rapidly. Even profitable positions should be exited by 14:30 on TIER1_NEAR contracts. This is not optional.
ℹ️Why 14:30 and not 15:25? For a NIFTY weekly option with 3 DTE, the theta per minute in the final hour is approximately 3–4× the theta per minute earlier in the day. A position worth ₹20 at 14:30 may be worth only ₹8–12 at 15:25 with zero spot movement. The liquidity also deteriorates as dealers reduce risk into close. Exit while the market is still orderly.
PHASE 6

End-of-Day Review (15:30+ IST)

After market close and all positions are closed, spend 10–15 minutes reviewing today's session. Improvement as a systematic trader comes from post-session analysis, not from trading more.

  • GEX Chart replay: At what time did GEX peak? When did it collapse below 70%? Did a large spot move correlate with that timing? Was the signal early enough to have traded?
  • Alert Feed review: All today's alerts are preserved. Were COC_VELOCITY alerts accurate? Were RETAIL_PANIC alerts followed by a bounce? Build your own statistics over weeks.
  • P&L attribution in Position Monitor: What percentage came from Delta vs Vega vs Theta? A vega-heavy gain means IV expansion was the main driver — less predictable. A delta-heavy gain means the spot move was the driver — more aligned with the thesis.
  • Gate accuracy check: Did you enter at Gate ≥4? Did the trade move in the predicted direction within the first 30 minutes? Over time, track your win rate vs Gate score to calibrate the system to your own trading style.
  • No-trade day validation: If Gate never reached 4 and you correctly did not trade, record this as a green day — capital preserved is as valuable as capital gained.
  • Close Terminal tabs. Data is safe on disk. No need to stop any database or clean up files. Everything persists safely.

Position Sizing Framework

The dashboard provides signals, but position sizing is entirely your responsibility. No signal eliminates the probability of loss. This framework ties lot size directly to the Environment Gate score and a defined maximum risk per trade.

Sizing by Gate Score

Gate ScoreRegimePosition FractionRationale
7–9Strong GO100% of planned sizeAll conditions aligned. Maximum edge. Deploy full allocation.
5–6GO with minor gaps75% of planned sizeStrong signal, one or two conditions not met. Slightly reduced.
4Borderline GO50% of planned sizeMinimum threshold. Half size only. Exit faster if Gate doesn't improve.
2–3WAIT0%Mixed signals. No edge. Waiting costs nothing; trading without edge costs capital.
0–1NO-GO0%Sit out entirely. The best trade on a NO-GO day is no trade.

Maximum Risk per Trade

Never risk more than 2% of total trading capital on any single option position. With the theta-adjusted SL set at a maximum 30% loss from entry, calculate your maximum lot size as:

Max_Risk_₹ = Total_Capital × 0.02
SL_Loss_₹ = Entry_LTP × 0.30 × Lot_Size
Max_Lots = floor(Max_Risk_₹ / SL_Loss_₹)

Example: Capital = ₹5,00,000. Entry LTP = ₹20. NIFTY lot size = 75 units.
Max_Risk = ₹10,000
SL_Loss per lot = ₹20 × 0.30 × 75 = ₹450
Max_Lots = floor(10,000 / 450) = 22 lots at full Gate score
At Gate score 4 (50% fraction): 11 lots
🛑
Daily Loss Limit

If you lose 3× the risk on a single trade (6% of capital) in one day, stop trading. Dashboard stays open for monitoring, but no new entries. Reset tomorrow.

📐
NSE Lot Sizes

NIFTY = 75 · BANKNIFTY = 30 · FINNIFTY = 65 · MIDCPNIFTY = 120 · NIFTYNXT50 = 25 units per lot. Check NSE website for updates — lot sizes change periodically.

These playbooks describe the ideal signal stack for the three most common session types. Use them as target patterns to recognise, not rigid rules to force.

Bearish Setup — Put Option Buying

IDEAL SIGNAL STACK · HIGH-CONFIDENCE DIRECTIONAL SELL SETUP

The ideal bearish setup occurs when institutional selling is visible in futures (CoC/V_CoC), market maker hedging support is collapsing (GEX), and retail traders are incorrectly positioned (PCR). All three simultaneously is rare and extremely high-conviction.

SignalTarget ReadingGate PtsStatus
V_CoC<−10 (entry), <−20 (maximum conviction)2Required — institutional selling confirmed
GEX % of Peak<70% (entry signal), <50% (full conviction)2Required — pinning force absent
CoC levelIn discount (futures below spot)1Strongly preferred — confirms sustained selling
PCR Divergence>+0.25 (retail panic puts = retail trap)1Optional but adds conviction
Options OBISmoothed <−0.151Optional — confirms sell-side aggression
IVP<50%2Required for max score — cheap puts preferred

Execution

  1. Gate ≥5 with V_CoC <−10 confirmed as anchor condition
  2. Screener: filter PE, sort S_score descending, select top with ρ <0.15 and delta −0.25 to −0.45
  3. Buy 75–100% of planned lots depending on Gate score
  4. Position Monitor: input expiry, strike PE, entry LTP, theta. SL plots immediately.
  5. Target: 100% gain. Book 50% at target. Trail remainder with theta SL.
Best Entry Window
09:30–10:15 IST. Institutional selling that started before open (visible in V_CoC) typically drives the main move in the first 90 minutes.
PE Skew Warning
PEs are always priced higher than equivalent CEs due to structural skew. If IVP >60%, the PE is already expensive. Reduce size or target a slightly OTM PE to reduce vega exposure.

Bullish Setup — Call Option Buying

IDEAL SIGNAL STACK · HIGH-CONFIDENCE DIRECTIONAL BUY SETUP

The ideal bullish setup mirrors the bearish setup exactly but in reverse. V_CoC >+10 signals institutional futures buying; GEX collapse removes the overhead dealer resistance; PCR divergence below −0.20 (retail panic-selling calls into a squeeze) adds contrarian confirmation.

SignalTarget ReadingGate PtsStatus
V_CoC>+102Required — institutional buying confirmed
GEX % of Peak<70%2Required — overhead resistance from dealers gone
CoC levelIn premium and widening—Confirming — not directly Gate-scored but adds conviction
PCR Divergence<−0.20 (retail panic-selling calls)1Optional — contrarian squeeze catalyst

CE vs PE asymmetry: Because IV skew makes PEs structurally more expensive, CEs at the same moneyness are relatively cheaper. The S_score for ATM CEs tends to be slightly higher than equivalent PEs on a flat-IV day, making strike selection easier for bullish plays.

Best CE Entry Window
09:30–10:30 IST. Bullish institutional buying often starts at open and continues through mid-morning. NIFTY makes its intraday high before 11:30 on strong bullish days.
Afternoon CE Warning
Buying CEs after 12:00 IST on a day that has already rallied significantly is high risk. The move is mature, theta accelerates, and reversal risk increases. Avoid new CE entries after noon unless a fresh breakout signal fires.

Chop Day — No-Trade Protocol

CAPITAL PRESERVATION · RECOGNISING UNFAVOURABLE CONDITIONS

A chop day is when the Environment Gate stays at 0–3 all session and no setup triggers. GEX remains high, V_CoC is flat, PCR shows balanced flows. Option premiums slowly bleed from theta. Recognising this early — and not forcing trades — is one of the highest-value skills the dashboard enables.

Chop Day Identification (by 10:30 IST)

GEX stays >80% of peak
Dealers heavily hedged and actively defending range. Market pinned. No breakout energy building.
V_CoC stays −5 to +5
No institutional campaign. FIIs inactive or perfectly balanced. No footprint visible in futures.
PCR Divergence stays ±0.15
Retail and institutional flows broadly aligned. No mis-positioning to exploit. No contrarian edge.
Gate stays at 0–3
Structural confirmation of no edge. The system is explicitly telling you: today is not a day to buy options.

Protocol for a Confirmed Chop Day

  • If Gate has not reached 4 by 10:30 IST, officially declare it a chop day. No new entries for the remainder of the session.
  • Use the day for passive learning: watch the screener update every 5 minutes, observe how S_scores shift as the session progresses, build intuition for what "normal" readings look like.
  • Review yesterday's or last week's high-conviction sessions using the date picker to reinforce pattern recognition.
  • Do not lower your Gate threshold. "4 is the minimum" is a system rule — not a guideline you adjust when bored.
  • Record in your journal: "Chop day confirmed 10:30. Gate stayed at X. No trades. Capital preserved."
