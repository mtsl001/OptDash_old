Dashboard v2.0

A real-time quantitative analytics platform for systematic NSE index option buying. It pulls live market data from BigQuery every five minutes, processes it through eight analytical modules, and surfaces actionable signals — from gamma exposure to slippage-adjusted strike selection — so you can trade with institutional-grade information.

Data Flow — Step by Step

Every five minutes during NSE market hours (09:16:30 → 15:30 IST) the pipeline executes this cycle:

  1. Load watermark
    Reads data/watermark.json — the timestamp of the last successfully pulled record. After backfill, this starts at approximately 2026-02-20 15:30:xx.
  2. Pull incremental rows from BigQuery
    Runs: SELECT * FROM upxtx WHERE record_time > {watermark} ORDER BY record_time ASC. Returns ~1,600 rows per 5-min snapshot covering all index option and futures contracts.
  3. Validate
    Checks schema, coalesces nulls in ltp using close_price then close. Corrects negative IV values. Logs row counts by instrument type.
  4. Load or compute ATM window
    Reads today's ATM windows from data/processed/atm_windows/YYYY-MM-DD.json. If first pull of the day, computes from the opening 09:15 spot price and saves the file.
  5. Compute derived columns
    Adds: gex_k, coc, moneyness_pct, oi_delta, expiry_tier, in_atm_window, obi_raw, snap_time, trade_date, and more.
  6. Write Parquet
    Saves as data/raw/options/trade_date=YYYY-MM-DD/UNDERLYING_HHMM.parquet. Uses row_group_size=100,000 and Snappy compression for maximum DuckDB query speed.
  7. Refresh DuckDB + update watermark
    DuckDB glob views auto-discover new Parquet files. Watermark advances to the max record_time in the batch and is atomically written to data/watermark.json.

Concept Library

Every metric on the dashboard is grounded in options market microstructure theory. This section explains each concept from first principles — what it measures, the formula, and what it means for an option buyer specifically.

Γ

Gamma Exposure (GEX)

MARKET STRUCTURE · DEALER HEDGING MECHANICS

Every time you buy an option from a market maker, they are now short that option. To avoid taking a directional bet (remaining "delta-neutral"), they must hedge by buying or selling the underlying index futures. Gamma tells you how much their hedge changes as the spot price moves by one point. GEX aggregates this across every single open contract across all strikes and expiries to produce a market-wide picture of the hedging forces dealers will exert on price.

Think of positive GEX as a gravitational force pulling price back toward the most crowded strike. When GEX is large and positive, every move away from equilibrium forces dealers to trade against it — buying dips and selling rallies — creating a "pinning" or mean-reversion effect. When GEX collapses or turns negative, that gravitational force disappears and directional moves become self-reinforcing.

Per-contract GEX contribution
GEX_k = Gamma × OI × Spot² × 0.01 × d_dir

d_dir = +1 for Call options   # dealers are short calls → long gamma
d_dir = −1 for Put options    # dealers are short puts → short gamma

The dashboard sums all GEX_k values across every Tier 1 strike and expiry to produce Net GEX in ₹ Billions for each 5-minute snapshot. The time series shows how this evolves through the day.

Positive GEX (Chop)
Dealers long gamma. They actively counter every price move. Market mean-reverts. Avoid momentum option plays.
Negative GEX (Trend)
Dealers short gamma. They must buy when price rises and sell when it falls — amplifying every move. Ideal for directional option buyers.
GEX Collapse (≤70%)
Even when positive, a rapid 30% drop in GEX removes the pinning support. Trending conditions begin here. This is the buy signal for options.
💡Best setup: GEX was high at market open (dealers heavily hedged, market pinned), then drops ≥30% by 09:45–10:00 as dealers unwind. Combined with V_CoC < −10, this marks a very high-probability directional move. Both conditions score 4 of the 9 possible Environment Gate points together.
₹

Cost of Carry (CoC) & Velocity

FUTURES MARKET · INSTITUTIONAL FOOTPRINT DETECTION

In a rational market, futures trade at a premium above spot equal to the risk-free financing cost for the holding period. For NIFTY near-month futures, this "fair carry" is typically +30 to +80 index points. When large institutional players aggressively buy or sell futures, they push prices away from this theoretical fair value — and Cost of Carry (CoC) is how we observe that pressure.

The key insight is that FIIs and large prop desks build futures positions before they show up in options or price action. The futures market has lower impact cost and greater liquidity, so it is their preferred vehicle for expressing a directional view. By the time price starts moving, you can already see the institutional intent in CoC and V_CoC.

Cost of Carry
CoC = Near-month Futures LTP − Underlying Spot

15-minute Velocity
V_CoC(t) = CoC(t) − CoC(t − 3)    # 3 bars × 5 min = 15 minutes

Fair carry (NIFTY near month): approximately +30 to +80 points
CoC below 0 = futures in discount = sellers dominating
V_CoC < −10
FIIs selling ₹500Cr+ equivalent in 15 min. Strongest early warning of a large down move. Often leads spot by 60–90 min. Gate +2 pts.
CoC in Discount
Futures below spot. Sustained discount + V_CoC < −10 = institutional short campaign actively running.
V_CoC > +10
Institutional buying campaign. Bullish structural shift. Favour CE buying. Opposite of the bear setup.

Why does V_CoC lead by 60–90 minutes? Institutions build futures exposure first (lowest market impact), then delta-hedge their total book by placing options orders. The options market sees the institutional flow last, but it shows up in futures CoC first. By monitoring V_CoC, you are effectively front-running the options flow.

σ

Implied Volatility (IV)

OPTIONS PRICING · THE PRICE OF UNCERTAINTY

Every option has a theoretical price derived from the Black-Scholes model, which requires an estimate of future volatility as an input. Implied Volatility is that estimate — reverse-engineered from the market's observed option price. It represents the market's consensus forecast of annualised standard deviation of the underlying's returns.

If NIFTY ATM options are pricing an IV of 14%, the market is saying: "We expect NIFTY to move approximately ±14% annually." For a 7-day option, this translates to a daily expected move of 25,000 × 14% / √252 ≈ ±220 points per day.

Daily expected move from IV
Daily 1σ Move = Spot × IV% / √252

Expected move to expiry
Move_to_expiry = Spot × IV% × √(DTE / 365)

As an option buyer, IV is your cost. When you buy a high-IV option, you need the underlying to move more than the market already expects — which is harder to achieve. When IV is low (IVP <30%), even a moderate directional move produces a profit because you bought cheap volatility.

Volatility Smile and Skew

In index options, put IVs are almost always higher than equivalent call IVs at the same distance from spot. This "negative skew" reflects institutional demand for downside protection. On the IV Surface panel, PE strikes appear as brighter (higher IV) cells than equidistant CE strikes. Understanding skew helps you recognise when puts are overpriced relative to calls and vice versa.

IV Low (IVP <30%)
Options historically cheap. Every point of movement earns more than it would at high IV. Best buying conditions.
IV Fair (IVP 30–65%)
Reasonable entry. Not optimal, not terrible. Size position conservatively.
IV High (IVP >75%)
Paying premium for volatility that is likely to compress. Avoid naked longs. Use debit spreads instead.

IV Rank (IVR) & IV Percentile (IVP)

VOLATILITY CONTEXT · TIMING INDICATOR

Knowing that IV is at 14% tells you nothing without context. Is 14% cheap or expensive for NIFTY historically? IVR and IVP both answer this by placing today's IV within its recent history — but they do so differently, and the difference matters.

IV Rank (IVR) — position within the range
IVR = (IV_today − IV_90d_low) / (IV_90d_high − IV_90d_low) × 100

0% = at 90-day low    100% = at 90-day high

IV Percentile (IVP) — fraction of days below today
IVP = (Count of past 90 days where IV_close < IV_today) / 90 × 100

25% IVP = IV was lower than today on 75 of the past 90 days
80% IVP = today is more expensive than 80% of recent days
IVR — simple but sensitive to outliers

One extreme event (crash spike, earnings) distorts the entire range. If IV hit 38% during a crash 60 days ago and is now at 16%, IVR might read 15% even though 16% is elevated for normal conditions. Use IVR as a quick reference, not for precise timing.

IVP — robust and preferred

Not affected by outliers because it counts days, not range position. A crash spike just adds one day to the count. IVP is more stable and reliable for the Environment Gate condition. The dashboard uses IVP for scoring.

IVP RangeLabelAction for option buyers
<20%CHEAPStrong buy environment. Volatility at historically low levels. Maximise position size.
20–50%FAIRReasonable entry. Environment Gate scores 2 pts. Normal position sizing.
50–75%EXPENSIVECaution. Reduce size. Consider debit spreads to reduce vega exposure.
>75%VERY EXPENSIVEAvoid naked long options. IV very likely to revert lower, hurting your position even on correct direction.
ℹ️IVP requires at least 7 days of historical closing IV data to produce a meaningful reading. In the first week after backfill, this condition will show "insufficient data" and not score points in the Environment Gate. It becomes fully reliable after ~30 days of live data.
P/C

Put-Call Ratio (PCR) & Divergence

SENTIMENT ANALYSIS · RETAIL vs INSTITUTIONAL

The Put-Call Ratio measures the relative activity in put options versus call options. Traditionally a ratio >1 signals bearish sentiment. The dashboard goes much deeper by tracking two separate PCRs — one measuring current activity (retail) and one measuring accumulated positioning (institutional) — and comparing them.

PCR Volume — fast, intraday retail sentiment
PCR_Vol = Σ PE_volume / Σ CE_volume    # this 5-min snapshot only

PCR OI — slow, accumulated institutional positioning
PCR_OI = Σ PE_open_interest / Σ CE_open_interest    # built over days

PCR Divergence — the signal
Divergence = PCR_Vol − PCR_OI

Why they diverge and what it means: Open Interest accumulates slowly over days as institutions patiently build positions. Volume spikes intraday when retail traders react to news, fear, or broker calls. When a sudden retail rush to buy puts (PCR_Vol spike) occurs while institutions remain relatively neutral in OI (PCR_OI steady), the divergence tells you that retail panic is occurring against a calm institutional backdrop — historically marking a local price low, not a trend continuation.

Divergence > +0.30
Retail panic-buying puts while institutions hold calls steady. Classic retail trap. Spot tends to bounce within 1–4 candles. Gate +1 pt. Contrarian CE signal.
Divergence < −0.20
Retail piling into calls while institutions hold puts. Spot tends to fall. Contrarian PE signal.
Balanced (−0.20 to +0.25)
Retail and institutional flows broadly aligned. No strong contrarian signal. Look to other indicators for direction.
Δ

Option Greeks

PRICE SENSITIVITY MEASURES

The Greeks quantify how sensitive an option's price is to changes in underlying factors. They are pre-computed by the BigQuery data source and used throughout the dashboard for GEX, S_score, P&L attribution, and stop-loss computation.

GreekMeasuresPractical meaning for option buyersTypical ATM range
Delta (Δ) ₹ change per 1-pt spot move ATM option Δ ≈ 0.50. NIFTY moves +100 pts → option gains ≈₹50. Delta increases toward 1.0 as option goes deeper ITM. Think of it as the option's "speed" relative to spot. 0.20 – 0.80
Gamma (Γ) Rate of Δ change per 1-pt move High gamma means your delta accelerates on favourable moves — a convex payoff. ATM options have maximum gamma. Gamma collapses rapidly in the last 2–3 days before expiry for OTM options. 0.0001 – 0.005
Theta (Θ) ₹ lost per day from time alone The daily "rent" you pay to hold the option. If Θ = −6.63, you lose ₹6.63/day even if spot stays flat. Accelerates exponentially in the final week before expiry. Always negative for option buyers. −0.5 to −30 (near-expiry ATM)
Vega (ν) ₹ change per 1% IV change IV rising from 14% to 15% with Vega=12 → option gains ₹12. Option buyers benefit from IV expansion (vega positive). IV compression after a spike hurts buyers even when direction is correct. 5 – 50 for index ATM

Taylor Series P&L Decomposition

The Position Monitor decomposes your P&L using a second-order Taylor expansion around the option's price function. For each 5-minute bar:

ΔV ≈ Δ·ΔS + ½Γ·(ΔS)² + ν·Δσ + Θ·Δt

ΔS = spot price change in this bar
Δσ = implied volatility change in this bar
Δt = 1/75 (each 5-min bar = 1/75 of the 375-minute trading day)
Unexplained = Actual ΔV − Theoretical ΔV (model error, higher-order terms)

This breakdown tells you why your position made or lost money. If your delta P&L is positive but vega P&L is heavily negative, it means IV compressed against you even as price moved in your favour — a common frustration in high-IV environments.

S★

Slippage-Adjusted S_score

STRIKE SELECTION · EFFICIENCY RANKING

The S_score answers: "Among all available strikes, which gives the best ratio of directional exposure per unit of time decay, after accounting for the real-world cost of execution?" It is the primary ranking criterion in the Strike Screener.

Step 1: Efficiency Ratio (directional gain per unit decay cost)
EffRatio = |Delta| / |Theta|

Step 2: Slippage Penalty ρ (order book quality)
ρ = exp(−2 × TBQ × TSQ / ((TBQ + TSQ) × V_bench))

TBQ = total buy quantity in order book
TSQ = total sell quantity in order book
V_bench = 2,000,000 (benchmark for index options)

ρ → 0 when book is deep and balanced (negligible slippage)
ρ → 1 when book is thin or one-sided (severe slippage)

Step 3: Final S_score
S_score = EffRatio × (1 − ρ)

The slippage penalty ρ uses the harmonic mean of bid and ask side quantities as a measure of book health. A deep, balanced book has high TBQ × TSQ relative to V_bench — so the exponent approaches zero, ρ approaches zero, and S_score equals the raw efficiency ratio. A thin or lopsided book is penalised severely, protecting you from entering positions that look attractive on paper but will cost you several percent in impact cost.

S_scoreStar RatingInterpretation
>0.06★★★★Excellent. High delta-per-theta with liquid execution. Priority candidates.
0.04 – 0.06★★★☆Good. Solid efficiency with acceptable liquidity.
0.02 – 0.04★★☆☆Fair. Moderate efficiency. Worth tracking but not first choice.
<0.02★☆☆☆Poor. Either inefficient Greeks or illiquid book. Avoid unless specific reason.

Order Book Imbalance (OBI)

MICROSTRUCTURE · REAL-TIME PRESSURE

Order Book Imbalance measures the balance of live buy versus sell pressure in the order book at any moment. Unlike volume (which records what already traded), OBI reflects what is queued to trade — making it a leading indicator of short-term price pressure.

OBI = (Total Buy Qty − Total Sell Qty) / (Total Buy Qty + Total Sell Qty)

Range: −1 to +1
+1.0 = all bids, zero asks (extreme buy pressure)
−1.0 = all asks, zero bids (extreme sell pressure)
0.0 = perfectly balanced

Smoothed OBI (5-snapshot rolling average)
Smoothed_OBI(t) = Average(OBI(t), OBI(t−1), OBI(t−2), OBI(t−3), OBI(t−4))

The dashboard computes OBI per row using total_buy_qty and total_sell_qty from the BigQuery schema. A 5-snapshot (25-minute) rolling average removes noise and reveals sustained pressure. This smoothed OBI feeds Environment Gate condition 6 (for options) and condition 3 (for futures).

Smoothed OBI < −0.15
Sustained sell pressure in options order book. Sellers are more aggressive and willing to hit bids. Gate +1 pt.
Smoothed OBI > +0.15
Buy-side pressure dominant. Buyers aggressively lifting offers. Consistent with upward momentum.
|OBI| < 0.10
Balanced order flow. No directional pressure signal. Market in equilibrium or low activity.
SL

Theta-Adjusted Trailing Stop Loss

RISK MANAGEMENT · POSITION MONITORING

A conventional percentage stop loss (e.g. "exit if premium drops 30%") ignores a fundamental property of options: premiums naturally decay from time alone, even when the underlying doesn't move. A tight fixed stop will frequently exit valid positions due to normal theta decay — not because the trade was wrong.

The Theta-Adjusted SL solves this by lowering the stop loss floor in proportion to time elapsed and the option's known daily theta. This way, you are only stopped out by an adverse spot move exceeding what theta explains — not by the clock ticking.

Fixed base stop loss (set at entry)
SL_base = Entry_Premium × (1 − Max_Loss_Fraction)
Example: ₹19.95 × (1 − 0.30) = ₹13.97

Theta-adjusted SL at elapsed time t
SL_adj(t) = SL_base + (Theta_daily × t_elapsed_min / 375)

375 = total NSE trading minutes (09:15:00 to 15:30:00)
Theta_daily is negative → SL_adj moves lower as time passes

Worked example: You buy a NIFTY PE at ₹19.95 with Theta = −6.63/day, Max_Loss = 30%.

  • At entry (09:20): SL_base = ₹13.97. SL_adj = ₹13.97 + 0 = ₹13.97
  • At noon (12:00, 160 min elapsed): Theta component = −6.63 × 160/375 = −2.83. SL_adj = ₹13.97 − ₹2.83 = ₹11.14
  • If PE is trading at ₹22 at noon: ₹22 > ₹11.14 → IN_TRADE. Stop not hit.
  • If PE is trading at ₹10 at noon: ₹10 < ₹11.14 → STOP_HIT. An adverse spot move caused this, not theta.

Expiry Tiers

LIQUIDITY CLASSIFICATION · TRADING UNIVERSE

NSE lists options for multiple expiry dates simultaneously — weekly, monthly, and quarterly. Not all expiries have the same liquidity or analytical value. The dashboard classifies every contract into one of three tiers based on Days-to-Expiry (DTE) and applies tier-specific logic throughout analytics.

TierDTE RangeUse in DashboardTypical Contracts
TIER1_NEAR ≤ 12 days Primary: GEX, CoC, PCR, Strike Screener, Position Monitor. Highest liquidity, tightest spreads, sharpest gamma response. Current week + next week NIFTY weekly expiry
TIER1_FAR ≥ 35 days Secondary: GEX (included), term structure charting. More stable theta profile. Less gamma sensitivity. Monthly expiry, next monthly expiry
TIER2_IV_ONLY 13–34 days IV surface and term structure charts only. Wide spreads, low OI. Not suitable for trading. Excluded from GEX and screener. Biweekly or mid-month expiries
ATM

ATM Window

STRIKE UNIVERSE · DAILY ANCHOR

The ATM Window defines which strikes are included in all dashboard analytics for each underlying. It is anchored to the day-open spot price — not the current live price. This keeps the strike universe consistent throughout the day, regardless of intraday moves.

ATM strike (from first 09:15 snapshot)
ATM = ROUND(Day_Open_Spot / Strike_Interval) × Strike_Interval

Window bounds
Lower = ATM − (8 × Strike_Interval)
Upper = ATM + (8 × Strike_Interval)

NIFTY (interval=50): 17 strikes over 800-point range
BANKNIFTY (interval=100): 17 strikes over 1,600-point range
MIDCPNIFTY (interval=25): 17 strikes over 400-point range

The window file is computed from the first snapshot of each day and saved to data/processed/atm_windows/YYYY-MM-DD.json. All subsequent pulls that day reuse this fixed window — the dashboard shows the same strike universe from 09:15 to 15:30 even if the spot has moved 300 points.

ℹ️If NIFTY gaps up significantly at open, the ATM window shifts to reflect the new opening price — not yesterday's close. A 400-point gap-up would shift the entire NIFTY window up by 8 strikes.
M%

Moneyness

STRIKE POSITION RELATIVE TO SPOT

Moneyness describes where a strike price sits relative to the current spot price, expressed as a percentage. The dashboard uses a continuous moneyness definition that works consistently across all underlyings regardless of their absolute price level.

Moneyness% = (Strike_Price − Underlying_Spot) / Underlying_Spot × 100

For Call options (CE):
  Moneyness > 0% → OTM (strike above spot, not yet in the money)
  Moneyness ≈ 0% → ATM
  Moneyness < 0% → ITM

For Put options (PE): the same formula applies; interpretation reverses
  Moneyness < 0% → OTM for puts (strike below spot)

The Strike Screener uses moneyness to identify near-ATM options (|moneyness| <1%) for the best liquidity, and the IV Surface uses it to align strikes across different underlyings on a comparable scale.

Trading Workflow

The dashboard is designed around a structured daily trading workflow. Following this sequence consistently is more important than any individual signal.

Phase 1: Pre-Market (09:00–09:15 IST)

  1. Open three Terminal tabs. Start pipeline (Tab 1), then API (Tab 2), then dashboard (Tab 3).
  2. Verify all three running: check :8000/docs loads and :5173 shows panels.
  3. Select yesterday's date in the dashboard date picker and review the closing GEX — was it rising or falling into close? What was the CoC trend? This sets your prior for today.
  4. Check IVP from the Strike Screener. If IVP <30%, you have a structural edge on every trade today. If IVP >75%, plan to size down or use spreads.
  5. Note any major pre-market news (RBI, FII data, global futures). Large gap opens shift the ATM window significantly.

Phase 2: Market Open Window (09:15–09:45 IST)

  1. 09:15: Market opens. First BigQuery snapshot forms. Pipeline pulls it at 09:16:30. ATM window computes automatically from the opening spot price.
  2. 09:20: First full snapshot on dashboard. Check CoC: is futures already in discount? Is V_CoC negative on the very first reading? If yes, institutional shorts were placed pre-market — high-alert bearish day.
  3. 09:20–09:30: Strike Screener panel populates with S_score rankings. Note the top 3–5 strikes by S_score for CE (bullish plays) and PE (bearish plays) separately. Do not enter yet.
  4. 09:30–09:45: Watch GEX % of peak. If it drops rapidly from the opening value, the pinning force is collapsing fast — a strong setup signal. Wait for Environment Gate to reach 4+ before entering.
  5. If Gate = GO by 09:30–09:45: Enter your chosen strike from the screener. Enter the position details into Position Monitor immediately.
  6. If Gate = WAIT at 09:45: Extend your wait. Re-check every 5 minutes. If Gate never reaches GO by 10:30, skip the day for new entries.
⚠️First-15-minutes rule: Never enter a trade between 09:15 and 09:30. The opening auction creates artificial liquidity and wide spreads. Prices printed in the first two candles are not reliable entry points. Always wait for at least the 09:20 snapshot to stabilise.

Phase 3: In the Trade (09:45–14:30 IST)

  1. Immediately after entry, input the position into the Position Monitor: expiry, option type, strike, entry snap time, entry LTP, and daily theta (from the Strike Screener table).
  2. The theta-adjusted SL line now plots automatically and updates every 5 minutes. Check it passively — the Position Monitor shows your current status (IN_TRADE, GUARANTEED_PROFIT_ZONE, etc.).
  3. Check the Alert Feed after every dashboard refresh. Any new COC_VELOCITY alert? Any GEX_DECLINE alert below 50%? These may require you to act, not just observe.
  4. Monitor PCR Divergence: if it reverses strongly against your position direction (e.g., you are long PE but PCR divergence swings sharply positive = retail panic puts = contrarian CE signal), consider exiting 25–50% of your position.
  5. At 100% profit (LTP = 2× entry): book 50% at market. Move remaining position to trailing SL at entry price. This creates a free trade where you cannot lose more than you've already booked.

Phase 4: Exit Rules (in order of priority)

EXIT 1
Stop Loss Hit: Position Monitor displays STOP_HIT in red. LTP dropped below the theta-adjusted SL. Exit the entire position immediately at market. Do not average down. Do not wait for a bounce. This is a system rule, not a suggestion.
EXIT 2
Signal Collapse: Environment Gate drops from GO to NO-GO (score falls to 0–1). The structural conditions that justified your entry have reversed. Exit 75–100% of position immediately. The edge is gone.
EXIT 3
Partial on Signal Weakening: Gate drops from GO to WAIT (score 2–3). Conditions mixed. Exit 50% to reduce risk. Keep remaining 50% with tighter stop (raise SL manually to entry premium).
EXIT 4
Profit Target: LTP reaches 2× entry (100% gain). Book 50% at market. Move remaining to guaranteed-profit trailing mode by raising SL to entry price.
EXIT 5
Time Exit — 14:30 IST: Close 100% of all positions by 14:30 IS
