A
Environment Gate
8-condition pre-flight checklist · Score 0–11 (6 core + 2 VEX/CEX bonus) pts · GO / WAIT / NO-GO verdict

The Environment Gate is the most important panel on the dashboard. It is a quantitative pre-flight checklist that evaluates whether the current market structure is suitable for buying options. Six independent conditions are tested in real-time; each condition earns weighted points. The total score determines whether you should trade.

Rule 1: Never enter a new position when the Gate score is below 4. This is the single most important discipline in the system. On NO-GO days, even the best-looking setups on other panels should be ignored entirely.

The 6 Gate Conditions

# Condition Pts What It Measures Signal Logic
1 GEX declined >30% from daily peak 2 Market maker hedging force is weakening High GEX forces mean-reversion. Once GEX drops 30%+ from its daily high, the pin is released and directional moves become viable for option buyers.
2 V_CoC < −10 at any point today 2 FII/institutional aggressive futures selling A V_CoC reading below −10 means futures carry velocity turned sharply negative — an institutional bearish campaign is underway (selling futures aggressively to build a short position).
3 Futures OBI negative 1 Order book tilted to sell side in futures More sellers than buyers showing in the futures order book. Confirms that supply pressure is real and not just price-based — the book imbalance is visible before price moves.
4 PCR divergence >0.25 1 Retail buying puts while institutions stay flat When volume-PCR diverges significantly from OI-PCR, retail traders are piling into puts while institutional OI is flat. This asymmetry often signals a retail trap (a drop that reverses) or confirms institutional direction.
5 IVP < 50% 2 Options are cheap relative to recent history IV Percentile below 50 means current IV is below the 90-day median. Buying options below median IV is structurally advantageous — you pay less theta per delta, and mean-reversion of IV (expansion) works in your favour.
6 Smoothed OBI < 0 1 Options order book tilted to seller side A 5-snapshot moving average of the options OBI (buy qty vs sell qty) being negative means structural sell pressure in the option order book — consistent with a directional environment favourable for buyers.

How to Read the Gate Panel

The panel displays the total score prominently as a circular ring gauge (0–11 scale) with a colour: red for NO-GO, amber for WAIT, green for GO. Below the ring, six condition rows show individual pass/fail status. Colour coding: green checkmark = condition met, red X = condition not met. Each row shows the condition name, the points earned, and a brief status phrase.

Day-Type Patterns

Best setup day

GEX drops from morning peak by 10:00, V_CoC hits −15 by 09:30, IVP is 30%. All 6 core conditions fire (+ 2 VEX/CEX bonus = 8 total). Gate can reach 11. This is the day to trade at maximum size in the direction V_CoC points.

Typical NO-GO day

GEX is at or near daily peak all day, V_CoC hovering between −5 and +5, IVP is 70%+. Gate stays at 2–3 all session. Any "interesting" move in spot is a trap — the market maker pinning force dominates.

⚠️The Gate is a real-time system, not a morning checkpoint. It is re-evaluated every 5 minutes. A day that starts as NO-GO can turn to GO by 11:00 if GEX drops and V_CoC fires. Conversely, a GO morning can revert to WAIT after 13:00 if conditions deteriorate. Keep the dashboard visible throughout the session.
B
Net GEX — Gamma Exposure
Intraday timeseries · % of daily peak · Regime classification · Dealer hedging direction

Net GEX (Gamma Exposure) measures the aggregate gamma position of all market makers across the ATM window. It tells you how forcefully dealers are hedging — and therefore how much they are suppressing or amplifying directional price moves.

The GEX panel shows a line chart of net GEX across every 5-minute snapshot today. The Y-axis is in raw GEX units (₹ Cr equivalent). A horizontal line marks the daily peak (highest intraday reading). The current GEX as a percentage of that peak is displayed prominently — this is the number Gate condition #1 is watching.

What the Chart Shows

Chart Element Meaning
Blue line — Net GEX timeseries Aggregate GEX across all market makers. Plotted at each 5-min snapshot. Rising = dealers accumulating gamma (pinning force increases). Falling = dealers reducing/unwinding gamma (pinning force decreases).
Dotted reference line — Daily peak The highest GEX reading recorded today. All "% of peak" calculations are relative to this line. Resets at 09:15 each trading day.
% of peak badge Current GEX ÷ daily peak GEX × 100. Gate condition #1 fires when this drops below 70% (GEX declined > 30% from peak).
Regime badge Text label: LONG GAMMA, SHORT GAMMA, NEUTRAL, or TRANSITIONAL. Computed from GEX sign and magnitude relative to 30-day norms.
Net GEX sign Positive = dealers are long gamma (hedging against moves = pinning). Negative = dealers are short gamma (hedging with moves = amplifying).

GEX Regimes and What They Mean for Option Buyers

LONG GAMMA (High Positive)
Pinning Environment
Dealers are net long gamma. Every up-move forces dealer selling; every down-move forces dealer buying. The market oscillates in a narrow band. Buying options is very difficult — directional moves fail at dealer rebalancing points. Avoid new entries.
TRANSITIONAL (Declining from Peak)
Pre-Breakout Window
GEX is falling from its daily high (30–70% of peak). The dealer hedging force is diminishing. Price can begin to trend without as much resistance. Gate condition #1 fires here. Begin active monitoring for entry.
NEUTRAL / LOW
Directional Window
GEX is below 50% of peak. Dealer pinning force is minimal. Price can trend strongly. This is when option premiums expand fastest. Best environment for long options.
SHORT GAMMA (Negative)
Amplified Moves
Dealers are net short gamma. They must buy into up-moves and sell into down-moves — which amplifies volatility. Very fast trending moves possible. High reward but also fast reversals. Only trade with very tight stops.

Reading the GEX Chart: Key Patterns

Peak at Open → Falling All Day

GEX peaks at 09:20, then steadily declines. By 10:30 it's at 55% of peak. By 11:30 it's at 35%. This is a textbook opportunity day — the pin is systematically unwinding and momentum can build through the session.

GEX Spikes Mid-Session

GEX rises sharply mid-day (e.g., at 11:00). This usually indicates a large options position being established (calendar spread or long strangle by a dealer). Expect price to get pinned to the new GEX peak zone for the remainder of the session. Exit positions or tighten stops.

ℹ️GEX Direction vs Direction of Trade: Net GEX does not tell you which direction to trade — it only tells you whether directional trading is viable. For direction, use CoC Velocity (Panel C). GEX enables the trade; V_CoC directs it.
C
CoC Velocity — Cost of Carry
Futures–spot spread · 15-min rolling velocity · Institutional directional bias detector

Cost of Carry (CoC) is the premium (or discount) at which futures contracts trade relative to the spot index. In a normal market, futures trade at a premium to spot (the "fair carry"). When FIIs aggressively buy or sell futures, the carry changes faster than fair value — and the velocity of that change reveals their direction.

Cost of Carry Formula
CoC_t = Futures_LTP_t − Underlying_Spot_t

15-Minute Velocity Formula
V_CoC_t = CoC_t − CoC_(t−3) /* 3 snapshots × 5 min = 15-min change */

What the Panel Shows

Visual Element What It Represents
Upper chart — CoC absolute The raw futures premium vs spot across the day. Two reference lines: fair carry upper bound (+₹30–35 for NIFTY) and fair carry lower bound (−₹5). Readings outside this band indicate structural positioning beyond arbitrage.
Lower chart — V_CoC bars Green bars = positive velocity (carry increasing → futures buyers are dominant). Red bars = negative velocity (carry decreasing → futures sellers are dominant). Height = magnitude of the 15-min change in carry.
V_CoC badge Current V_CoC reading in points. Gate condition #2 triggers when this reads < −10 at any intraday snapshot.
Trend arrow Up/down arrow indicating whether V_CoC has been net positive or negative over the last 30 minutes. More reliable directional signal than a single snapshot.

Signal Interpretation

V_CoC Reading Interpretation Action
Below −10 FIIs aggressively selling futures. Institutional bearish campaign confirmed. This velocity typically precedes a significant spot move down within 60–90 minutes. Gate condition #2 fires. Look for Put entries via Strike Screener.
−5 to −10 Moderate futures selling. Direction is bearish but not at conviction level yet. Monitor for further deterioration. Wait for −10 threshold before sizing. Keep at 50% if entering.
−5 to +5 Carry is changing slowly — no institutional conviction in either direction. The futures market is balanced. Directional entries are speculative. No Gate condition fires from V_CoC. Use other signals.
+5 to +10 Moderate futures buying. Institutions accumulating long futures. Bullish bias emerging but not at conviction level. Monitor for CE setups. Not enough for Gate trigger alone.
Above +10 FIIs aggressively buying futures. Bullish institutional campaign. Spot move up expected within 60–90 minutes. Gate condition fires (bullish version). Look for Call entries.

The 60–90 Minute Lead-Time

The most important characteristic of V_CoC is its forward-looking nature: institutional futures positioning changes before spot price responds. This happens because large investors build futures positions gradually to minimise market impact. The futures carry change is visible in V_CoC while the spot move is still 60–90 minutes away.

Worked Example — 21 Feb 2026, NIFTY

09:20
V_CoC = +2
09:25
V_CoC = −3
09:30
V_CoC = −7
09:35
V_CoC = −12 ← Gate fires
09:40
V_CoC = −14
11:00
↘ Spot begins sustained decline of 120 pts

Institutional futures selling was visible in V_CoC at 09:35 (−12). The resulting spot move started approximately 85 minutes later at 11:00. Entry at 09:40 on a NIFTY PE would have captured the full move with a head start.

Fair Carry Reference Band

The absolute CoC reading has its own significance. Each underlying has an expected fair carry band based on risk-free rate minus dividend yield, scaled by DTE of the near-month futures:

Underlying Normal CoC Band (near-month) Reading Below Band Reading Above Band
NIFTY +₹15 to +₹35 Futures at discount → strong institutional short Futures at excess premium → strong institutional long
BANKNIFTY +₹80 to +₹180 Below ₹80 → institutional short campaign Above ₹180 → institutional accumulation
FINNIFTY +₹20 to +₹50 Below ₹15 → bearish institutional bias Above ₹55 → bullish buildup
💡Best combined signal: When Gate score ≥ 5, GEX is below 60% of peak, AND V_CoC < −10, you have all three alignment signals simultaneously. This is the ideal setup described in the Bearish Playbook in Part 7. Strike selection via the Screener (Part 4) completes the trade decision.
