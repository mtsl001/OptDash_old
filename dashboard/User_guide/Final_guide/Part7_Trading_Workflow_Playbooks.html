Workflow &
Strategy Playbooks

This is the operational core of the guide — how to translate every concept and panel reading into an actual, sized trade, managed from entry to exit. Follow the 5-phase workflow every day. When a clear setup emerges, run the matching playbook.

7.1

The 5-Phase Daily Workflow

Each phase has a defined time window, a goal, and a specific checklist. The phases are sequential — never jump to Phase 3 without completing Phase 2. The discipline of the checklist is what separates systematic trading from reactive trading.

08:45 – 09:14 IST · Pre-Market
Phase 1 — System Verification & Context Setting

Before the pipeline pulls even one row from BigQuery, verify the stack is healthy and orient yourself to the macro context.

  • Confirm all three terminals are running: Pipeline (first pull starts at 09:16:30), API (uvicorn healthy), Dashboard (npm run dev on port 5173).
  • Check data/watermark.json — timestamp should be from last trading day at ~15:30.
  • Note any overnight SGX/Gift Nifty movement (large gap open will affect ATM window validity).
  • Note any known catalysts for today: RBI announcement, quarterly results, global macro events.
  • Set your maximum loss budget for the day in your trading journal before markets open.
09:15 – 09:30 IST · Market Open
Phase 2 — Opening Snapshot Analysis

The first 5-minute pipeline pull establishes the ATM window and computes the day's GEX baseline. Do not enter any trade during this phase.

  • Confirm ATM Window panel shows the correct ATM for each underlying (check against opening spot).
  • Note the baseline Net GEX level for each underlying — this becomes the "daily peak" reference for Gate condition #1.
  • Note IVP levels — if IVP >70% on open, that is an options-expensive day. Reduce planned lot size by 25%.
  • Check PCR levels — if PCR_vol and PCR_oi are already diverged by >0.25 on open, retail is already positioned. Record the divergence direction.
  • Open Strike Screener — note which strikes are showing the highest S_scores for both CE and PE. These are your candidates for Phase 3.
09:30 – 10:45 IST · Primary Entry Window
Phase 3 — Entry Decision

Entries taken in this window benefit from maximum DTE remaining in the day and tend to capture the primary institutional move before full market participation. This is the highest-value window for the strategy.

  • Wait for Gate score ≥ 4 before considering any entry.
  • Confirm V_CoC has crossed −10 (bearish) or +10 (bullish) — this gives you direction.
  • Run the entry decision table: Gate score 7+ = 100% lots; 5–6 = 75%; 4 = 50%.
  • Open Strike Screener filtered to your direction (CE or PE). Select the top-rated strike (highest S_score) with IVP <50% and spread <5%.
  • Check Alert Feed for any GATE_CHANGE or VCOC alerts in the last 15 minutes.
  • Place order at LTP or within spread. Record entry price, time, and lot count in Position Monitor.
  • Confirm the Position Monitor SL chart has populated correctly — you should see the initial SL_t = SL_base.
10:45 – 14:30 IST · Active Hold
Phase 4 — Position Management

Once in a trade, your only jobs are to monitor the exit signals and to avoid second-guessing a position that is within its normal range. The Position Monitor handles the analytics — trust it.

  • Check Position Monitor every 5-minute candle: confirm LTP is above SL_t.
  • If LTP falls to or below SL_t at any point — exit immediately. No exceptions. Do not widen the stop.
  • If Gate score falls from GO to WAIT/NO-GO — reduce position by at least 50%.
  • If V_CoC reverses (was −15, now +5) — exit the matching directional position.
  • If a GATE_CHANGE alert fires in the Alert Feed — reassess urgently.
  • Do not enter a second position in the same underlying while the first is open (no averaging).
  • 14:30 rule: if the position is below breakeven at 14:30, exit regardless of signals.
14:30 – 15:30 IST · Wind-Down
Phase 5 — Exit & End-of-Day Protocol
  • No new entries after 14:30. Market close risk is too high for fresh positions.
  • All open positions must be closed before 15:25 — NSE closing session starts at 15:25 with very wide spreads.
  • Note the day's final GEX level, Gate score, and PCR for the trading journal.
  • After 15:30, verify the pipeline completed the 15:30 pull — watermark should show today's date at ~15:30.
  • Review P&L attribution in Position Monitor export — note which Greek drove the majority of today's P&L (delta, theta, vega). This builds intuition over time.
  • Shut down cleanly: Ctrl+C pipeline, Ctrl+C API, Ctrl+C dashboard. Confirm all three terminals clear.
7.2

Lot Sizing Framework

Lot sizing is the bridge between signal quality and capital deployment. The framework ensures that maximum capital is deployed only when all conditions are the most favourable — and minimum capital (or none) is deployed when conditions are marginal.

Gate Score V_CoC IVP Size (% of Planned Lots) Rationale
7–9 <−10 or >+10 <50% 100% All three primary conditions met: regime, direction, cost.
7–9 <−10 or >+10 50–70% 75% Strong Gate + direction, but IV slightly expensive. Reduce vega risk.
5–6 <−10 or >+10 <50% 75% Good conditions but Gate not peak. One reduction tier.
5–6 <−10 or >+10 50–70% 50% Moderate Gate + expensive IV. Half-size only.
4 Any confirming <50% 50% Borderline GO. Minimum entry only.
4 Neutral (−10 to +10) Any 0% No directional conviction. No entry.
0–3 Any Any 0% WAIT or NO-GO. Absolute prohibition.
Any Any >70% 0% IV too expensive. Options buying structurally unfavourable regardless of Gate.
💡Rule of thumb: "Planned lots" is the number you decide before markets open, based on your day's capital allocation. The table then applies the appropriate fraction. Never plan more than your total risk budget divided by the maximum expected loss per lot (which is Entry_Price × Lot_Size × SL_fraction).
7.3

Non-Negotiable Rules

These rules apply without exception regardless of conviction, news, or past performance. Each was designed to prevent the most common ways systematic option buyers lose capital.

  1. No entry when Gate < 4. Ever. Not for "just a small scalp", not because you "feel" the market. If the Gate is not GO, there is no trade.
  2. No averaging into a losing position. If LTP falls toward SL_t, the position is wrong. Do not buy more. The move you expected has not materialised — the bias must be reassessed.
  3. No stop loss widening after entry. You set SL_base at entry based on your risk tolerance. You may not widen it under any circumstances. Widening a stop under pressure is how small losses become large losses.
  4. No new positions after 14:30. The risk-reward ratio for new positions in the final hour is unfavourable due to theta acceleration and closing auction liquidity.
  5. No positions held overnight. Close all positions by 15:25. The model provides no overnight context (no futures rollover data, no global open modelling). Overnight gap risk is unquantifiable in this framework.
  6. No trading on NSE holidays or after daily daily maximum loss. Respect the trading calendar. Once your daily maximum loss budget is hit, stop for the day — no revenge trading.
  7. No buying options with IVP > 70%. This is a structural constraint, not a suggestion. You are fighting IV headwinds that will statistically erode your edge.
PB1

Playbook 1 — Institutional Breakdown

📉
Institutional Breakdown — Bearish PE Setup
Trigger: V_CoC < −10 + GEX decline >30% + Gate ≥ 5

The highest-conviction setup in the system. FIIs are actively building futures shorts (V_CoC < −10), dealer hedging is weakening (GEX dropped 30%+ from peak), and the Environment Gate confirms all other conditions are aligned. The index is structurally positioned to decline and the option buying window is open.

Entry Conditions — All Must Be True

  • V_CoC has crossed −10 (VCOC_BEARISH alert fired) and has sustained below −10 for at least 10 minutes
  • Net GEX has fallen >30% from today's opening peak (Gate C#1 fired)
  • Gate score ≥ 5 (ideally 7+)
  • IVP <50% (options are cheap)
  • PCR divergence direction: Vol PCR ≥ OI PCR (retail buying puts = trap setup) OR OI PCR already heavy with puts (institutional structural short)

Trade Structure

  • Buy ATM or 1 strike OTM PE on the underlying where signals are strongest (typically NIFTY or BANKNIFTY)
  • Expiry: TIER1_NEAR with DTE 3–7 (enough time but maximum delta efficiency)
  • Target: 2× entry premium (i.e., if you paid ₹80, target exit at ₹160)
  • SL: Dynamic SL_t from Position Monitor; do not override
  • Time limit: exit by 14:30 regardless of P&L

Exit Signals

  • LTP falls to SL_t → exit immediately
  • V_CoC reverses above −5 → exit 50% of position
  • Gate score drops below 4 → exit
  • Target hit → exit full position
  • 14:30 → exit regardless
PB2

Playbook 2 — Gamma Unwind + Bullish Confirmation

📈
Gamma Unwind + Bullish Futures — CE Setup
Trigger: V_CoC > +10 + GEX < 50% of peak + Gate ≥ 5

The bullish mirror of PB1. Institutional desks are accumulating futures long positions (V_CoC turning strongly positive), dealer hedging has diminished (GEX well below peak), and the index has structural tailwinds. Retail sentiment may be lagging (call-PCR not yet elevated), giving you early-mover advantage.

Entry Conditions — All Must Be True

  • V_CoC > +10 and rising (VCOC_BULLISH alert fired)
  • Net GEX < 50% of today's peak OR GEX trend consistently declining through the morning session
  • Gate score ≥ 5
  • IVP <50%
  • Futures OBI positive (buy-side dominant — confirming futures accumulation)

Trade Structure

  • Buy ATM or 1 strike OTM CE on the strongest underlying by V_CoC magnitude
  • Expiry: TIER1_NEAR DTE 3–7
  • Target: 2× entry premium
  • SL: Dynamic SL_t; trail it as position profits

Exit Signals

  • LTP falls to SL_t → exit
  • V_CoC reverses below +5 → exit 50%
  • IV_SPIKE alert fires (post-move IV crush risk) → exit full position
  • Target hit or 14:30 → exit
PB3

Playbook 3 — Pure Low IV Environment

💎
Pure Low IV — Volatility Expansion Play
Trigger: IVP < 25% (extreme bottom) + IV_CRUSH pattern in recent sessions

When IVP is in the bottom quartile (<25%), any significant market move — regardless of direction — will be amplified by IV expansion. This is a "buy cheap volatility and let it expand" play rather than a pure directional trade. Direction selection is still guided by V_CoC, but position sizing is more aggressive because the IV tailwind provides a structural edge.

Entry Conditions

  • IVP < 25% for the target underlying — confirmed on IV Surface panel (all strikes uniformly cool)
  • Gate score ≥ 4 (minimum Gate threshold, since IV edge compensates for some Gate weakness)
  • V_CoC provides direction; if neutral, consider a straddle-like approach by buying both ATM CE and ATM PE (very rare, only when IVP < 15%)

Trade Structure

  • Buy ATM strike of target underlying; expiry TIER1_FAR (DTE 13–25) to maximise vega exposure
  • Use slightly higher lot size than usual because the IV tailwind improves expected value
  • Target: 40–60% premium gain (not just 2× — low IV entry means smaller absolute moves produce larger % gains)

Important: Post-Entry IV Monitoring

If IV expands significantly (IV_SPIKE alert) after entry, consider exiting on the IV spike rather than waiting for spot to move further. The vega cushion has been captured — selling into the expansion locks in the structural IV profit before any mean-reversion.

PB4

Playbook 4 — Event-Driven IV Trade

⚡
Event-Driven Trade — Known Catalyst (RBI / Results)
Trigger: Known scheduled event within 1–2 trading days + IVP <50% in FAR expiry while NEAR expiry is elevated

Event-driven plays require a different setup: the IV Surface should show an inverted term structure (near-term expiry IV inflated by event risk, far-term IV still moderate). Instead of buying the near-term option (expensive), you buy the next expiry to avoid the inflated event premium but still benefit from the post-event directional move that often continues for 1–2 days.

Setup Identification

  • IV Surface panel shows near-expiry IVP >70% but far-expiry IVP <50%
  • Known catalyst: RBI policy date, election results, major quarterly result (Reliance, TCS, HDFC Bank)
  • V_CoC provides a pre-event lean: if institutional desks are already positioned bearish (V_CoC < −10), the post-event continuation is likely bearish even if there is a knee-jerk bullish spike initially

Trade Structure

  • Buy TIER1_FAR expiry ATM or 1-strike OTM in the V_CoC direction
  • Entry on the day before the event, typically in Phase 3 window, only if Gate ≥ 4
  • Plan to hold through the event and exit the day after, capturing the continuation move once IV settles
  • Hard SL: 40% of entry premium (wider than usual because next-day continuation expected)
⚠️IV crush risk: If the event outcome is priced in and the post-announcement move is smaller than expected, even a correct directional bet can lose money due to IV crush. Monitor vega P&L in Position Monitor continuously. If vega P&L exceeds delta P&L gain within 30 minutes of announcement, exit immediately — IV is crushing faster than spot is moving.
