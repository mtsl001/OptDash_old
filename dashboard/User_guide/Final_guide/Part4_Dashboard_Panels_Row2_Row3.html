D
Strike Screener
All ATM-window strikes ranked by S_score · Slippage-adjusted entry selection

The Strike Screener ranks every eligible option in the current ATM window (±8 strikes from ATM, both CE and PE, across TIER1 expiries only) by S_score — a composite metric that balances delta efficiency, theta burden, bid-ask slippage cost, and order book quality. It removes guesswork from strike selection entirely.

Screener Columns Explained

Column Data Shown Ideal Value (for buying)
Strike Strike price —
Type CE or PE badge CE for bullish/V_CoC >+10; PE for bearish/V_CoC <−10
Expiry Expiry date · DTE in parentheses TIER1_NEAR with DTE 3–12 for momentum; TIER1_FAR for swing
LTP Last traded price —
Delta Option delta (absolute value) 0.30–0.45 for balance of cost vs directional sensitivity
Theta Daily theta (negative value) Smallest absolute value = least time decay cost per day
EffRatio Δ / |Θ| — delta earned per unit of theta paid Higher is better. Above 0.10 is acceptable; above 0.20 is excellent.
OBI Order Book Imbalance −1 to +1 Positive for CE buying (more buyers visible); negative for PE buying
Spread% Bid-ask spread as % of LTP <3% ideal; <5% acceptable; >7% avoid (slippage too high)
IVP IV Percentile at this strike (0–100%) <40% preferred (cheap options)
S_score Final composite score (0–100) Higher is better. Top 3 rows are your best entry candidates.
Rating Star rating (★ to ★★★★★) Trade 4★ or 5★ only on borderline Gate days; any rating works on 7+ Gate days.

S_score Formula (Summary)

S_score Calculation
EffRatio = |Delta| / |Theta|
ρ (slippage penalty) = 1 − min(Spread% / 10, 1)
S_score = EffRatio × ρ × IVP_adjustment × OBI_adjustment × 100

/* IVP_adjustment: <50%=1.0, 50–70%=0.8, >70%=0.6 (penalises expensive IV) */
/* OBI_adjustment: directionally aligned OBI boosts score; contrary OBI reduces it */

Star Rating Thresholds

Stars S_score Range Interpretation When to Trade
★★★★★ 80–100 Exceptional: cheap IV, tight spread, high delta efficiency, aligned OBI Always — maximum size on Gate 7+
★★★★★ 65–79 Strong: most factors favourable Gate ≥ 5 · size at plan
★★★★★ 50–64 Acceptable: adequate quality with some compromise Gate ≥ 6 only · reduce size
★★★★★ 35–49 Below standard: spread or IV too high Gate 7+ only, if no better strikes exist
★★★★★ 0–34 Poor: do not trade this strike Never regardless of Gate score

Screener Workflow — How to Use it in Practice

  1. Confirm Gate score ≥ 4 (Panel A).
  2. Identify direction from V_CoC (Panel C): V_CoC < −10 → look at PE rows only; V_CoC > +10 → CE rows only.
  3. In the Screener, filter to the relevant option type (CE or PE) using the type filter toggle.
  4. Select the expiry tier: TIER1_NEAR for an intraday trade; TIER1_FAR if you plan to hold 2–5 days.
  5. The top row (highest S_score) is your primary entry candidate. Note the strike and expiry.
  6. Check the IVP column — if IVP > 60%, consider going one strike further OTM where IVP is lower, even if S_score is slightly lower.
  7. Confirm the OBI is positive (for CE) or negative (for PE). An opposed OBI is a caution signal.
  8. Execute the trade at the displayed LTP ± the spread. Do not chase if you miss the initial price.
⚠️ATM Window is fixed at day open. The screener only shows strikes within the ±8 window computed from the 09:15 spot price. If spot gaps up 300 pts on open, the window may be fully OTM for puts. In gap days, manually assess whether strikes are viable before entering — the screener cannot account for structural gap conditions.
E
PCR Divergence
Volume-PCR vs OI-PCR · Retail vs institutional sentiment · Divergence signal

The PCR Divergence panel tracks two different Put-Call Ratios simultaneously and measures the gap between them. The divergence between volume-PCR (retail activity) and OI-PCR (structural positioning) is a powerful sentiment indicator.

Volume PCR (Retail Sentiment)
PCR_vol = Total Put Volume / Total Call Volume (across ATM window)

OI PCR (Institutional Positioning)
PCR_oi = Total Put OI / Total Call OI (across ATM window)

Divergence Signal
PCR_div = |PCR_vol − PCR_oi| → Gate fires if > 0.25

Panel Elements

Chart Element Meaning
Blue line — OI PCR Structural positioning. Changes slowly — represents the cumulative options market position. More stable and reliable as a trend indicator.
Orange line — Volume PCR Same-day trading activity. More volatile — reflects retail crowd sentiment and reaction to news. Can spike wildly on event-driven moves.
Grey shaded band — Divergence The absolute gap between the two lines. The Gate condition #4 threshold (0.25) is marked as a dotted reference line.
Divergence badge Current PCR_div value. Text turns red when > 0.25 (Gate condition active).

Divergence Signal Interpretation

Vol PCR > OI PCR (Retail buying Puts)

Retail is actively buying more puts than calls today (high volume PCR), but structural OI is not confirming the bearish case. Two interpretations: (1) Retail trap — retail is wrong and the market reverses upward; (2) Early retail lead — rare but retail occasionally front-runs institutional action. Use V_CoC to disambiguate.

OI PCR > Vol PCR (Institutions building Puts)

Structural OI shows put dominance but today's volume is not confirming it. This means options were written or bought in previous sessions — earlier institutional positions are in put territory while current retail activity is neutral/bullish. Often a bearish structural backdrop even without acute day pressure.

ℹ️PCR alone is not an entry signal. It is a Gate condition contributor and a sentiment context indicator. Always combine PCR divergence with GEX regime and V_CoC before making a directional decision.

Alert Feed
Chronological triggered signals · Severity levels · Suggested actions per alert type

The Alert Feed is a timestamped event log of every analytical signal that crossed a threshold during the current trading session. It is not a recommendation engine — it is a notification layer that surfaces significant market microstructure events as they occur, so you can assess them in context of the broader dashboard state.

Alert Types & Their Significance

Alert Type Chip Trigger Condition Suggested Action
GEX_DROP_30 📉 GEX Net GEX falls below 70% of daily peak for the first time Gate condition #1 now active. Begin Phase 2 analysis (screener review). First alert of the day is the most significant.
VCOC_BEARISH 📉 V_CoC V_CoC drops below −10 for the first time that session Gate condition #2 active. Institutional bearish campaign likely underway. Prioritise PE entries if Gate ≥ 4.
VCOC_BULLISH 📈 V_CoC V_CoC rises above +10 Institutional bullish campaign. Prioritise CE entries. Confirm with GEX being below 70% of peak.
OI_SURGE_PUT 🔺 OI Put OI at a specific strike increases >50% in a single snapshot Large position being established in puts. Bearish institutional signal. Note the strike — it may become a key support/target level.
OI_SURGE_CALL 🔺 OI Call OI at a specific strike increases >50% in a single snapshot Large call writing or buying. If near ATM, may indicate resistance level/pin target. If far OTM, less significant.
IV_SPIKE ⚡ IV ATM IV rises >15% in a 15-minute window Sudden vol expansion — often precedes a large directional move. If you hold a long option, mark-to-market just improved. If entering, check IVP — buying into an IV spike is expensive.
IV_CRUSH 💧 IV ATM IV falls >15% in a 15-minute window (post-event) If you hold long options, exit immediately or reduce size. IV crush destroys long option value faster than delta loss. Classic post-RBI/FOMC/results scenario.
GATE_CHANGE 🚦 Gate Gate score crosses a tier boundary (e.g., WAIT → GO or GO → NO-GO) Most critical alert. A NO-GO trigger means close or hedge open positions immediately. A GO trigger means proceed with entry workflow.
STRIKE_BEST_CHANGED 🔄 Screener Top-ranked strike in Screener changes (S_score leader changes) If you have not entered yet, review the new top strike. If already in a trade at the previous top strike, this is informational only.
💡Alert sequencing matters. A GEX_DROP_30 followed within 15 minutes by VCOC_BEARISH is the ideal setup sequence. Both Gate trigger conditions firing in quick succession on the same underlying confirms a high-conviction directional entry window is open.

Key Signals from the IV Surface

Pattern Description Option Buyer Implication

Position Monitor
Live P&L · Theta-adjusted trailing SL chart · P&L attribution · Status labels

The Position Monitor is the right half of Row 3 (paired with IV Surface). After entering a trade, you manually input the position details once — then the panel calculates and displays live P&L, a dynamic trailing stop loss that adjusts for theta decay, and a decomposition of P&L into its Greek drivers.

Position Input Fields

Field What to Enter
Underlying NIFTY, BANKNIFTY, FINNIFTY, MIDCPNIFTY, or NIFTYNXT50
Strike The strike price you bought (must be within the ATM window)
Option Type CE or PE
Expiry Expiry date of the contract
Entry Price The premium you paid per unit (the LTP at time of entry)
Lots Number of lots (1 lot = NSE lot size for that underlying)
Entry Time HH:MM when you entered — used for theta tracking in the SL chart

The Theta-Adjusted Trailing Stop Loss Chart

The SL chart is the most technically differentiated feature of the Position Monitor. Unlike a fixed % stop loss that ignores the passage of time, this SL adjusts downward (becomes more permissive) as the day progresses and theta erodes the natural option value.

Base SL (Initial Stop)
SL_base = Entry_Price × (1 − SL_fraction) /* SL_fraction ≈ 0.30 (configurable in config.py) */

Theta Adjustment (at time t)
Theta_accrued_t = |Theta| × (t − entry_time) / 24hrs

Dynamic SL at time t
SL_t = max(SL_base − Theta_accrued_t, SL_base × 0.50)

In plain language: the stop loss floor falls by the amount of theta that has been earned since entry, preventing a premature stop-out that would be triggered purely by time decay rather than an adverse price move.

SL Chart Walk-Through

09:40 · Entry at ₹82
SL_base = ₹82 × 0.70 = ₹57.40. Chart shows flat stop line.
10:30 · 50 min elapsed · Theta = −₹2.50/day
Theta accrued = ₹2.50 × (50/960) ≈ ₹0.13. SL_adjusted = ₹57.40 − ₹0.13 = ₹57.27. Barely changed — stop stays firm early.
13:00 · 4+ hours elapsed
Theta accrued ≈ ₹1.04. SL_adjusted = ₹57.40 − ₹1.04 = ₹56.36. SL has drifted down by ₹1 — gives the position room to breathe through mid-session noise.
15:00 · 5+ hours elapsed (late session)
Theta accrued ≈ ₹1.56. SL_adjusted = ₹57.40 − ₹1.56 = ₹55.84. Floor protection kicks in at 50% of SL_base (₹28.70), but position has not dropped that far — the trailing stop respects the theta-neutral floor.

P&L Attribution Panel

Below the SL chart, the monitor shows real-time P&L decomposed by Greek driver:

Component What It Shows When It Dominates P&L
Delta P&L Profit/loss from spot price movement (Δ × spot_change × lots × lot_size) Dominant when spot is moving strongly in your direction
Gamma P&L Profit from accelerating delta as spot moves (½ × Γ × spot_change² × lots × lot_size) Becomes significant in large, fast moves (>100 pts in <30 min)
Theta P&L Loss from time decay (Θ × elapsed_hours/24 × lots × lot_size) Dominates on slow/flat days. The primary cost of option buying.
Vega P&L Profit/loss from IV changes (Vega × IV_change × lots × lot_size) Significant after event-driven IV moves. IV expansion is a bonus for long options.
Net P&L Total mark-to-market (LTP − entry) × lots × lot_size Always shown. Must match sum of Greek components approximately.
✅Exit trigger: Exit the position when: (1) LTP falls to or below SL_t; (2) Gate score drops to WAIT/NO-GO; (3) V_CoC reverses direction sharply; (4) 14:30 arrives with the position at breakeven or below (no new entries after 14:30 rule). See Part 7 for the complete exit protocol.

Vanna / Charm Exposure (VEX/CEX)
Panel I · Row 3 (right column) · GET /micro/vex-cex · polled every 5 s

What It Shows

VEX/CEX quantifies the two mechanical dealer hedging forces that move NIFTY in directions "unexplained by news" — especially in the 45 minutes before weekly expiry known as Dealer O'Clock (14:45–15:25 IST).

  • VEX (Vanna Exposure): When implied volatility drops, put deltas shrink toward zero. Dealers who were short puts (positive delta) must buy back futures to stay neutral. Positive VEX = invisible bid; negative VEX = hidden ceiling on rallies.
  • CEX (Charm Exposure): As time passes, OTM deltas decay toward zero. Dealers must systematically buy back futures each day — largest on expiry day 09:15–15:25.

Formulas (computed in processor.py)

vanna       = -φ(d1) × d2 / sigma        # standard Black-Scholes, sigma in decimal
charm_daily = charm_annual / 365

# Per-strike exposure stored in Parquet (M-units = millions)
vex_k = OI × vanna × spot × sigma × d_dir / 1e6
cex_k = OI × (-charm_daily) × d_dir / 1e6

d_dir = +1 for CE,  -1 for PE
Positive cex_k → dealers must BUY futures → bullish mechanical flow

⏰ Dealer O'Clock

Thursday 14:45–15:25 IST (minutes 885–925 since midnight). In this window, dealers must close or roll all delta hedges as expiring options die. CEX becomes explosive — this is the mechanical explanation for why NIFTY moves 80–120 pts against all apparent news in the last 45 min before weekly close. The panel shows an orange badge when active.

Gate Integration

VEX/CEX feeds two bonus Gate conditions (conditions #7 and #8):
