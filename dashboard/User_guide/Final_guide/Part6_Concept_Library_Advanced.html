Advanced Concepts:
The Analytical Engine

These concepts power the computational sections of the dashboard — the S_score ranking, OBI conditions, theta-adjusted stop loss, expiry tier classification, and the ATM window. Understanding them helps you interpret why the system makes the rankings it does, and when to override them.

C6

Option Greeks ScreenerPosition Monitor

The Greeks are partial derivatives of the option price with respect to different market inputs. They quantify exactly how much your option's value will change for a given change in spot, time, or volatility. The dashboard uses all four first-order Greeks in its analytics.

Δ
Delta
Price sensitivity
Definition
Δ = ∂V / ∂S (change in option price per ₹1 change in spot)

ATM range: CE delta ≈ +0.45 to +0.55. PE delta ≈ −0.45 to −0.55. OTM options: lower absolute delta. ITM: higher. Practical meaning: If you buy 1 lot of NIFTY PE with delta = −0.40 and NIFTY falls 100 pts, your option gains ≈ ₹40 × 50 (lot size) = ₹2,000 in delta P&L, before other Greeks.

Γ
Gamma
Delta acceleration
Definition
Γ = ∂Δ / ∂S = ∂²V / ∂S² (rate of change of delta per ₹1 spot move)

ATM range: 0.0005 to 0.002 for NIFTY options. Practical meaning: As an option buyer, you have positive gamma — your delta grows in your favour as spot moves toward you, and shrinks as it moves against you. This is the fundamental advantage of long options over futures. In a fast move, gamma makes option gains super-linear.

Θ
Theta
Time decay cost
Definition
Θ = ∂V / ∂t (change in option price per 1 calendar day elapsed)

ATM range: −₹2 to −₹8/day for NIFTY near-expiry options. Theta is always negative for long options. Practical meaning: The daily "rent" you pay for holding the option. An option with Θ = −3 loses ₹3/day from time alone. With 50 lots and a lot size of 25, that is ₹3,750/day. Theta accelerates significantly in the final 5 DTE.

V
Vega
IV sensitivity
Definition
ν = ∂V / ∂σ (change in option price per 1% change in IV)

ATM range: ₹5 to ₹15 per 1% IV change for NIFTY ATM options. Practical meaning: When IV expands after you buy, Vega multiplies your gain. When IV crushes post-event, Vega can wipe delta gains. This is why buying options only when IVP <50% structurally positions you for IV expansion rather than compression.

Taylor Decomposition of Option P&L

Full Greek P&L Decomposition (second-order approximation)
ΔV ≈ Δ × ΔS + ½ × Γ × ΔS² + Θ × Δt + ν × Δσ

where ΔS = spot change, Δt = elapsed time (fraction of day), Δσ = IV change
This is exactly what the Position Monitor's P&L attribution shows — broken into its four components.
C7

S_score — Strike Selection Score Screener

S_score is the dashboard's proprietary composite metric for ranking option strikes. It synthesises four factors into one score from 0 to 100: delta efficiency per unit theta, slippage penalty from bid-ask spread, IV cost adjustment, and order book direction alignment.

Step 1 — Efficiency Ratio (delta per theta)
EffRatio = |Delta| / |Theta|
Higher EffRatio means more directional sensitivity for less daily time cost. An option with Δ=0.40 and Θ=−2.0 has EffRatio=0.20. An option with Δ=0.30 and Θ=−3.5 has EffRatio=0.086. The first is 2.3× more efficient.

Step 2 — Slippage Penalty Factor (ρ)
ρ = 1 − min((Spread% / 10), 1.0)
Spread% = (Ask − Bid) / LTP × 100. If spread is 2%: ρ = 1 − 0.2 = 0.80. If spread is 7%: ρ = 1 − 0.7 = 0.30. Wide spreads heavily penalise the score.

Step 3 — IVP Adjustment
IVP_adj = 1.0 if IVP < 50% | 0.8 if 50–70% | 0.6 if >70%

Step 4 — OBI Alignment Adjustment
OBI_adj = 1.15 if OBI aligned | 1.0 if neutral | 0.85 if contrary

Final S_score
S_score = EffRatio × ρ × IVP_adj × OBI_adj × 100 (clipped to 0–100)

Worked Example

Input Value Factor Applied
Delta 0.38 EffRatio = 0.38 / 2.4 = 0.158
Theta −2.4
Bid-Ask Spread% 2.5% ρ = 1 − 0.25 = 0.75
IVP 38% IVP_adj = 1.0
OBI −0.12 (PE sell pressure aligned) OBI_adj = 1.15
S_score 0.158 × 0.75 × 1.0 × 1.15 × 100 = 13.6 → 2 stars
ℹ️A 2-star S_score in this example reflects the slightly wide spread (2.5%) dragging down an otherwise decent EffRatio. If the spread tightens to 1.5% mid-session, the score rises to 18.2 (3 stars). Always recheck the screener 5–10 minutes before entry as spread conditions change throughout the day.
C8

Order Book Imbalance (OBI) Gate C#3 & C#6Screener

OBI quantifies the net directional pressure visible in the market's limit order book, measured as the ratio of buy quantities to sell quantities in the top-5 bid-ask levels.

Raw OBI per Snapshot
OBI_raw = (total_buy_qty − total_sell_qty) / (total_buy_qty + total_sell_qty)
Result ranges from −1.0 (100% sell-side) to +1.0 (100% buy-side). Zero = perfectly balanced.

Smoothed OBI (5-snapshot moving average · Gate Gate C#6)
OBI_smooth = Average(OBI_raw over last 5 snapshots = 25 minutes)

Gate Conditions Using OBI

Condition OBI Source Threshold What It Measures
Gate C#3 Futures OBI (raw) < 0 Futures order book has more sell quantity than buy quantity → supply pressure in futures confirming bearish institutional flow
Gate C#6 Options OBI (smoothed, 5-snap avg) < 0 Options order book persistently sell-side dominated → reduces option writer resistance to directional moves, confirming buyer-favourable environment
S_score Per-strike OBI (raw) Directional alignment OBI in the same direction as your planned trade (negative for PE, positive for CE) boosts S_score by 15%. Contrary OBI penalises by 15%.
⚠️OBI can be spoofed — large orders placed and immediately cancelled inflate the visible qty temporarily. The 5-snapshot smoothing in Gate C#6 mitigates this by requiring the imbalance to persist for 25+ minutes, not just a single snapshot.
C9

Theta-Adjusted Trailing Stop Loss Panel H

A conventional percentage stop loss treats all time periods equally — a 30% loss at 10:00 triggers the same way as a 30% loss at 14:30. But for a long option, these are not equivalent situations: by 14:30, theta has already consumed several rupees of value, so the option's fair price at breakeven is structurally lower than at entry. The theta-adjusted SL accounts for this by moving the stop level down as time passes, exactly by the amount of theta earned since entry.

SL_base — Initial Stop
SL_base = Entry_Price × (1 − SL_fraction)
SL_fraction is set in config.py (default 0.30 = 30% loss stop)

Cumulative Theta Accrued at time t
Theta_accrued_t = |Theta_at_entry| × (minutes_elapsed / 960)
960 = total trading minutes in a 6.25-hour NSE session (09:15–15:30)

Dynamic SL at time t
SL_t = max(SL_base − Theta_accrued_t, SL_base × 0.50)
The 0.50 floor prevents the SL from drifting so low that it becomes meaningless. SL cannot fall below 50% of the original SL_base.

Why This is Correct Risk Management

Without the theta adjustment, you would stop out roughly at the same premium level throughout the day. But the market knows the option is worth less at 14:00 than at 09:30 simply due to time. A price equal to SL_base at 14:00 actually represents a larger real loss (relative to fair value) than the same price at 09:30, because theta has eroded the base. The adjustment is the mathematically correct way to maintain a constant real loss tolerance rather than a constant nominal tolerance.

C10

Expiry Tiers Screenerprocessor.py

Each option row is classified into one of three expiry tiers based on its DTE (days to expiry). Tiers determine which options appear in the Screener and how aggressively they are ranked.

TIER1_NEAR
DTE: 1–12
Near-term weekly/monthly expiry. Maximum gamma & delta sensitivity per rupee of premium. High theta cost. Ideal for intraday to 2-day trades.
Fully scored in Screener.
TIER1_FAR
DTE: 13–34
Mid-term expiry. Lower gamma but manageable theta. Suitable for 3–7 day swing trades. Less sensitive to intraday spot moves.
Fully scored in Screener with DTE penalty.
TIER2_IV_ONLY
DTE: 35+
Far-term or calendar options. Too much DTE to be relevant for short-term directional option buying. Used only for IV Surface reference (term structure analysis).
Not shown in S_score Screener.

Dynamic Re-tiering

Tier classification is recomputed fresh at every 5-minute snapshot. An option that was TIER1_FAR on Monday morning with DTE=15 will become TIER1_NEAR by the following Wednesday (DTE=10). The screener automatically adjusts its treatment. There is no manual intervention needed — tier re-tiering is invisible to the user but critical to the accuracy of S_score rankings near expiry week.

⚠️DTE = 1 (expiry day): Options with DTE=1 have extremely high theta. The Screener scores these very low despite potentially high delta, because the theta risk is disproportionate. Avoid entering new positions in expiry-day options unless you are making extremely short-duration scalp trades exiting before 12:00.
C11

ATM Window Screeneratm.py

The ATM Window defines the universe of strikes eligible for display in the Screener. It is computed once at day-open and remains fixed for the entire trading day — preventing the Screener from dynamically chasing strikes as spot moves, which would create entry instability.

Step 1
Get Day-Open Spot
Use the spot price from the 09:15 snapshot (first 5-min pull). This is the "anchor" price for the day.
Step 2
Round to Strike Grid
Round to the nearest strike interval for the underlying. NIFTY=50, BANKNIFTY=100, FINNIFTY=50, MIDCPNIFTY=25.
Step 3
Set ±8 Window
ATM Window = 8 strikes below ATM to 8 strikes above ATM = 17 strikes total per underlying.

Moneyness ScreenerIV Surface

Moneyness Formula
moneyness_pct = (Strike_Price − Spot_Price) / Spot_Price × 100
Positive = strike is above spot (OTM for calls, ITM for puts)
Negative = strike is below spot (ITM for calls, OTM for puts)
moneyness_pct Zone Delta Range (CE/PE) Description
< −3% Deep ITM CE: 0.75–0.95 / PE: 0.05–0.25 High delta, low time value. Expensive. Behaves almost like spot.
−1% to −3% Slight ITM CE: 0.55–0.75 Good delta but more expensive than ATM. Higher capital requirement.
−0.5% to +0.5% ATM CE/PE: ~0.45–0.55 Highest gamma, maximum time value. Sweet spot for buying.
+0.5% to +2% Slight OTM CE: 0.25–0.45 Cheaper premium, less delta. Good risk:reward if momentum expected.
+2% to +4% OTM CE: 0.10–0.25 Very cheap, but needs large move to be profitable. High loss probability.
> +4% Deep OTM CE: <0.10 Lottery tickets. Maximum IVP skew penalty. Avoid for systematic buying.
